EXCRETORY SYSTEM

(Anatomy, Waste Elimination & Homeostasis)

Academic Information

Class: X onwards

Topic: The Excretory System

Concepts: Waste Elimination, Kidney Function, Homeostasis

About the Excretory System
  • The human excretory system is crucial for maintaining the body's internal balance by removing metabolic waste products and regulating fluid and electrolyte levels.
  • It consists of several organs and structures that work together to ensure the efficient elimination of waste.
Learning Outcomes
  • Understanding Excretion: Knowledge of the excretory system's anatomy and function is crucial for recognizing symptoms and conditions related to kidney and urinary tract health, such as infections, kidney stones, and urinary incontinence.
  • Educational Insight: Learning about the excretory system highlights the importance of waste elimination in maintaining homeostasis, emphasizing the integration of physiological processes in overall health.
Structure

🟤 Kidneys

  • Two bean-shaped organs located in the lower back.
  • Responsible for filtering blood to remove waste products and excess substances.
  • Produce urine, which is then transported to the bladder.

🔗 Ureters

  • Two tubes that transport urine from the kidneys to the bladder.
  • Use peristaltic movements to ensure the smooth flow of urine.

🟡 Bladder

  • A hollow, muscular organ that stores urine until it reaches a volume that triggers the urge to urinate.
  • Contracts to expel urine from the body.

🔘 Urethra

  • A duct that carries urine from the bladder to the external environment during urination.
  • In males, it also serves as a passageway for semen.
Working
  • The kidneys filter blood and remove waste products, excess water, and salts.
  • The filtered waste forms urine.
  • Urine travels from the kidneys to the bladder through the ureters.
  • The bladder stores urine until it is ready to be expelled.
  • During urination, urine passes through the urethra and leaves the body.
  • This process helps maintain fluid balance and removes harmful wastes from the body.
