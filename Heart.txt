About the Heart
</div>
<div class="card-body">
<ul>
<li>The heart is a vital organ with a crucial role in maintaining life through its function as a pump.</li>
<li>Located in the chest cavity.</li>
<li>Consists of four chambers and a complex network of valves and vessels that coordinate the circulation of blood throughout the body.</li>
</ul>

<!-- STRUCTURE -->
<div class="card purple">
<div class="card-header">
<i class="fas fa-heartbeat"></i>
Structure
</div>
<div class="card-body">
<ul>
<li>The heart is divided into four chambers: Right Atrium, Right Ventricle, Left Atrium, Left Ventricle.</li>
<li>It also contains four main valves that ensure unidirectional blood flow: Tricuspid Valve, Pulmonary Valve, Mitral Valve, Aortic Valve.</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>

<!-- FUNCTION OF EACH PART -->
<div class="card red full-width">
<div class="card-header">
<i class="fas fa-cog"></i>
Function of Each Part
</div>
<div class="card-body">

<div class="chamber-box">
<h4>🔵 Right Atrium</h4>
<ul>
<li>Receives deoxygenated blood from the body via the superior and inferior vena cava.</li>
<li>Pumps this blood into the right ventricle through the tricuspid valve.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="chamber-box">
<h4>🔵 Right Ventricle</h4>
<ul>
<li>Pumps deoxygenated blood to the lungs through the pulmonary artery for oxygenation.</li>
<li>The pulmonary valve prevents backflow of blood into the right ventricle.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="chamber-box">
<h4>🔴 Left Atrium</h4>
<ul>
<li>Receives oxygenated blood from the lungs through the pulmonary veins.</li>
<li>Passes this blood into the left ventricle through the mitral valve.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="chamber-box">
<h4>🔴 Left Ventricle</h4>
<ul>
<li>Pumps oxygen-rich blood to the entire body via the aorta.</li>
<li>Due to the higher pressure required to distribute blood throughout the body, the left ventricle has thicker, more muscular walls.</li>
<li>The aortic valve prevents backflow into the left ventricle.</li>
</ul>
</div>
