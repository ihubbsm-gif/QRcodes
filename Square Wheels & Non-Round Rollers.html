SQUARE WHEELS & NON-ROUND ROLLERS

(Non-Circular Wheels & Eccentric Motion)

Academic Information

Class: V onwards

Topic: Non-Round Wheels & Eccentric Motion

Concepts: Catenary Curves, Cam Mechanisms, Mechanical Design

Learning Outcomes
  • Understand the principles and mechanics of non-round wheels.
  • Explain the unique motion of non-circular wheels.
  • Explore their practical applications in engineering.
  • Appreciate innovative mechanical design solutions.
Science Involved
  • Non-circular (eccentric) wheels have varying radii instead of a constant radius.
  • They follow specific mathematical curves or profiles.
  • Their changing radius creates unique motion patterns.
  • They convert uniform rotational motion into irregular linear motion.
  • This conversion is achieved through the wheel's continuously changing radius during rotation.
Working and Observation

🟥 Square Wheels

  • A square wheel rolls on a specially designed corrugated surface.
  • The surface follows a catenary-shaped curve.
  • As the wheel moves, the axle remains at a constant height.
  • This allows the square wheel to roll smoothly without jerks or bumps.

🔵 Arc Wheels

  • Arc wheels have a circular arc profile instead of a constant radius.
  • Two arc wheels can support a level platform.
  • The platform moves horizontally without significant vertical movement.
  • The gap between the tabletop and platform remains nearly constant despite axle movement.
Applications
  • Cam Mechanisms: Control the motion of followers such as valves and pistons.
  • Customized Motion Control: Enable acceleration, deceleration, and stationary phases by modifying the cam profile.
  • Automotive Engines: Convert rotational motion into reciprocating motion.
  • Variable Displacement Compressors: Used for efficient fluid compression systems.
  • Hydraulic Pumps: Improve mechanical power transmission.
  • Crankshafts and Bicycle Gears: Used in specialized mechanical designs.
