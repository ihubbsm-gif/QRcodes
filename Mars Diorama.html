MARS DIORAMA

(Geology, Atmosphere & Exploration of Mars)

Academic Information

Class: IV onwards

Topic: Mars Diorama

Concepts: Martian Geology, Atmosphere, Rovers & Orbiters

Learning Outcomes
  • Understand the geological features and atmosphere of Mars.
  • Learn about the possibilities and challenges of human exploration of Mars.
  • Develop scientific storytelling and model-making skills through the Mars diorama.
  • Appreciate the interdisciplinary nature of Mars exploration involving astronomy, geology, biology, and engineering.
Science Explained

🌋 Geological Features

  • Mars contains large volcanoes such as Olympus Mons.
  • It has deep valleys like Valles Marineris.
  • Numerous impact craters, including Gale Crater, are found on its surface.

🌫️ Atmosphere

  • Mars has a thin atmosphere.
  • It is composed mainly of carbon dioxide (CO₂).
  • Small amounts of nitrogen, argon, and other gases are also present.

💧 Water and Life

  • Scientific evidence suggests that liquid water existed on Mars in the past.
  • This has led scientists to investigate the possibility of past or present life on the planet.
Working and Observations
  • Diorama Design: The model represents the Martian surface and environment, including volcanoes, valleys, craters, and proposed human habitats.
  • Scientific Representation: The diorama incorporates scientific information about Mars' geography, climate, and resources, helping visualize conditions on the planet and future exploration possibilities.
Observational Evidence

🤖 Mars Rovers

  • Curiosity and Perseverance rovers provide information about Martian rocks, soil, and climate.
  • Their findings help scientists understand Mars' history and habitability.

🛰️ Mars Orbiters

  • Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter and Mars Express capture detailed images of the Martian surface.
  • They monitor weather patterns and seasonal changes on Mars.
Applications
  • Study the geological history of Mars.
  • Investigate the planet's atmosphere and climate.
  • Search for evidence of past or present life.
  • Support planning for future human missions and settlements.
  • Enhance understanding of planetary exploration and space science.
