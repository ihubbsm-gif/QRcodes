SOURCES OF POWER

(Minerals and Energy Resources)

Academic Information

Class: 10

Book Name: Contemporary India – II

Chapter: Minerals and Energy Resources

Learning Objective
  • Understand the various sources of power used for generating electricity, including renewable and non-renewable energy sources.
  • Explore the principles behind different types of power generation.
  • Understand their applications, environmental impacts, and economic significance.
Principle Behind Exhibit
  • The exhibit demonstrates the various sources from which we derive power to run different applications.
  • It highlights the differences in technology, efficiency, and sustainability among various power sources.
Solar Power

Definition

Solar power is the conversion of sunlight into electricity using two main technologies: Photovoltaics (PV) and Concentrated Solar Power (CSP).

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the basic principles of PV and CSP technologies.
  • Learn about the photoelectric effect and how it facilitates solar energy conversion.

Applications

  • Rooftop solar panels for homes and businesses.
  • Solar farms for large-scale electricity production.
  • Portable solar chargers for off-grid and remote-area applications.
Wind Power

Definition

Wind power is the conversion of wind energy into electricity using wind turbines or into mechanical power using windmills and wind pumps.

Learning Objectives

  • Study the technology and mechanics behind modern wind turbines.
  • Explore historical uses and advancements of windmills and wind pumps.

Applications

  • Wind farms for renewable electricity generation.
  • Windmills for agricultural applications.
  • Wind pumps for water pumping in rural areas.
Fossil Fuels Power

Definition

Fossil fuel power refers to the conversion of chemical energy from coal, natural gas, and oil into thermal, mechanical, and electrical energy.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the combustion processes involved in fossil fuel energy conversion.
  • Explore the environmental and economic impacts of fossil fuel use.

Applications

  • Electricity generation in power plants.
  • Transportation fuels such as petrol and diesel.
  • Heating and industrial processes requiring high energy input.
Hydro Power

Definition

Hydro power is derived from the energy of falling or flowing water, typically harnessed through hydroelectric dams or watermills.

Learning Objectives

  • Study the principles of hydroelectric power generation.
  • Learn about historical and modern applications of watermill technology.

Applications

  • Hydroelectric dams for large-scale electricity production.
  • Watermills for mechanical power applications.
  • Small-scale hydro systems for rural electrification.
Geothermal Power

Definition

Geothermal power utilizes heat energy from within the Earth's crust for electricity generation or direct heating applications.

Learning Objectives

  • Study methods of geothermal energy extraction and conversion.
  • Understand the environmental benefits and challenges associated with geothermal power.

Applications

  • Geothermal power plants for electricity generation.
  • Heating systems for residential and commercial buildings.
  • Industrial processes requiring continuous heat supply.
Learning Outcome
  • Understand the working principles of various sources of power generation.
  • Differentiate between renewable and non-renewable energy sources.
  • Recognize the applications of different power sources in everyday life.
  • Appreciate the importance of sustainable energy resources.
  • Develop awareness about the environmental and economic impacts of power generation.
