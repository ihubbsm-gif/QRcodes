LIVER

(Anatomy, Function & Metabolism)

Academic Information

Class: IX onwards

Topic: The Liver

Concepts: Anatomy, Metabolism, Detoxification

About the Liver
  • The liver is a vital organ with a multifaceted role in maintaining the body's overall health and homeostasis.
  • Located in the upper right abdomen.
  • It is one of the largest organs and performs a wide array of functions essential for life.
Learning Outcomes
  • Health Understanding: Knowledge of liver function helps in recognizing symptoms of liver disease, such as jaundice or abnormal blood tests. Understand the importance of liver health.
  • Educational Value: Learning about the liver's roles in metabolism, detoxification, and bile production highlights its significance in overall bodily function. Understand the interconnection of various physiological processes.
Structure
  • The liver is divided into two main lobes.
  • These lobes are further subdivided into smaller functional units called lobules.
  • Each lobule is comprised of liver cells, or hepatocytes, arranged around a central vein.
  • The liver also contains a network of bile ducts and blood vessels.
  • Hepatic Artery – supplies oxygenated blood.
  • Hepatic Portal Vein – delivers nutrient-rich blood from the digestive tract.
Function of Each Part

🧫 Hepatocytes

  • These liver cells are responsible for a variety of metabolic processes.
  • Synthesize proteins such as albumin and clotting factors.
  • Detoxify harmful substances.
  • Produce bile, which is essential for the digestion and absorption of fats in the small intestine.

🟡 Bile Ducts

  • Transport bile produced by hepatocytes to the gallbladder and small intestine.
  • Bile helps emulsify fats, aiding in their digestion and absorption.

🩸 Hepatic Artery and Portal Vein

  • The hepatic artery provides oxygenated blood to the liver.
  • The portal vein delivers blood rich in nutrients absorbed from the digestive tract.
  • This dual blood supply supports the liver's extensive metabolic functions and detoxification processes.
