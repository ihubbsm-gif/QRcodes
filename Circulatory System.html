CIRCULATORY SYSTEM

(Heart, Blood Vessels & Blood Composition)

Academic Information

Class: X onwards

Topic: The Circulatory System

Concepts: Heart, Blood Vessels, Blood Composition

About the Circulatory System
  • The human circulatory system is essential for transporting blood, nutrients, gases, and waste products throughout the body.
  • It plays a vital role in maintaining homeostasis, ensuring that tissues receive necessary substances and that metabolic waste is removed efficiently.
Learning Outcomes
  • Understanding Circulation: Knowledge of the circulatory system helps in identifying and managing cardiovascular diseases, such as hypertension and coronary artery disease.
  • Educational Value: Learning about this system highlights the integration of physiological processes, emphasizing the importance of cardiovascular health in overall well-being.
Structure

❤️ Heart

  • The central pump of the circulatory system.
  • Consists of four chambers: Right Atrium, Right Ventricle, Left Atrium, Left Ventricle.
  • The right side of the heart handles deoxygenated blood and sends it to the lungs.
  • The left side pumps oxygenated blood to the rest of the body.

🔗 Blood Vessels

  • Include arteries, veins, and capillaries.
  • Arteries: Carry oxygen-rich blood away from the heart.
  • Veins: Return deoxygenated blood to the heart.
  • Capillaries: Facilitate the exchange of nutrients, oxygen, and waste products between blood and tissues.

🩸 Blood

  • A fluid connective tissue composed of Red Blood Cells (RBCs) – carry oxygen, White Blood Cells (WBCs) – fight infection, Platelets – aid in blood clotting, and Plasma – the liquid component that transports cells, nutrients, hormones, and waste products.
Working
  • The heart pumps deoxygenated blood from the body to the lungs for oxygenation.
  • Oxygen-rich blood returns from the lungs to the left side of the heart.
  • The heart then pumps oxygenated blood through arteries to all parts of the body.
  • Capillaries allow the exchange of oxygen, nutrients, and waste products between blood and body tissues.
  • Veins carry deoxygenated blood back to the heart.
  • This continuous circulation supplies cells with essential substances and removes metabolic wastes.
