POWER FROM SEA

(Minerals and Energy Resources)

Academic Information

Class: 10

Book Name: Contemporary India – II

Subject: Geography

Chapter: Minerals and Energy Resources

Learning Objectives
  • Explore different methods of harnessing energy from the sea.
  • Understand tidal, wave, and ocean thermal energy conversion (OTEC).
  • Learn how marine energy is converted into electricity.
  • Understand the potential of marine energy as a renewable resource.
  • Recognize the challenges of utilizing sea-based energy.
Principle
  • Sea-based power generation uses tidal, wave, and ocean thermal energy.
  • It harnesses the kinetic and thermal energy of seawater.
  • Water movement or temperature differences drive turbines.
  • Turbines operate generators to produce electricity.
  • It provides a renewable and sustainable source of energy.
Working
  • Tides, waves, or ocean temperature differences are utilized.
  • Water movement or heat energy drives turbines.
  • The turbines rotate mechanical shafts.
  • The rotating shafts drive generators.
  • Generators convert mechanical energy into electricity.
  • The generated power is supplied for various applications.
Applications
  • Generates electricity in coastal regions.
  • Powers remote and island communities.
  • Supports desalination plants.
  • Provides a renewable energy source.
  • Reduces dependence on fossil fuels.
  • Promotes sustainable energy development.
