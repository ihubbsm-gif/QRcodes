<div class="card-body">
<ul>
<li>Recognize that in a closed system (no external torques), the total angular momentum remains constant.</li>
<li>Identify how angular momentum plays a role in situations such as gyroscopes, ice skaters spinning faster by pulling in their arms, or the stability of planets in their orbits.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<!-- SCIENCE INVOLVED -->
<div class="card blue">
<div class="card-header">
<i class="fas fa-lightbulb"></i>
Science Involved
</div>
<div class="card-body">
<ul>
<li>If no external torque acts on a body in motion, angular momentum (moment of inertia × angular velocity) remains constant.</li>
<li>When pulling weights towards the body, the moment of inertia decreases, and speed increases, conserving angular momentum.</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>

<!-- PARTICIPATE & OBSERVE -->
<div class="card purple">
<div class="card-header">
<i class="fas fa-hand-pointer"></i>
Participate &amp; Observe
</div>
<div class="card-body">
<ul>
<li>Sit on the turntable with weights on both sides. Slide weights to the farthest point and have a friend slowly set the turntable in motion.</li>
<li>Slide the weights towards your body to increase speed and push them back to decrease speed.</li>
<li>Hold a cycle wheel straight at a right angle and tilt it left or right to rotate the turntable accordingly, demonstrating the gyroscope principle.</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>
