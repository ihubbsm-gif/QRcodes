GRAND SHUTTLE

(Electromagnetic Induction)

Academic Information

Class: VI onwards

Topic: Electromagnetic Induction

Concepts: Faraday's Law, Magnetic Fields, Transformers

Learning Outcomes
  • Explain the fundamental principles of electromagnetic induction.
  • Understand Faraday's law of electromagnetic induction.
  • Develop the ability to analyze practical applications of electromagnetic induction.
  • Relate electromagnetic induction to everyday technologies.
The Scientific Interpretation
  • The exhibit demonstrates the principle of electromagnetic induction.
  • Pressing the switch allows current to flow through one of the coils.
  • The current generates a changing magnetic field.
  • The changing magnetic field induces a current in the adjacent coil.
  • This follows Faraday's law of electromagnetic induction.
  • The interaction between the magnetic field and the induced current produces a force on the bobbin.
  • The generated force propels the bobbin from one end to the other.
Working and Observation
  • A curved plastic pipe contains a bobbin resting on one side.
  • Coils of wire are placed at both ends of the pipe.
  • The coils are connected by a bundle of wires inside the pipe.
  • The bobbin acts as the primary component of a transformer.
  • Each coil functions as a secondary component.
  • Press the switch to energize one of the coils.
  • The upper end of the coil and the lower end of the bobbin acquire the same polarity.
  • Like poles repel each other.
  • The bobbin is pushed rapidly to the opposite end of the pipe.
  • Observe the motion of the bobbin caused by electromagnetic induction and magnetic repulsion.
Applications
  • Transformers: Transfer electrical energy between circuits and modify voltage levels.
  • Induction Cooktops: Heat cookware directly using electromagnetic induction.
  • Wireless Charging: Charge compatible devices through induced electric current.
  • Induction Heating: Heat conductive materials for industrial applications such as brazing and soldering.
  • Electromagnetic Compatibility (EMC) Testing: Test electronic devices by inducing controlled electromagnetic fields.
