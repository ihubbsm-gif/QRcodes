Learning Outcomes
</div>
<div class="card-body">
<ul>
<li>Recognize that rotatory motion involves rotation about a fixed axis.</li>
<li>Identify angular velocity and angular acceleration.</li>
<li>Understand the concept of moment of inertia.</li>
<li>Distinguish between linear and angular motion.</li>
<li>Explain the concept of centripetal force.</li>
<li>Understand why centripetal force is necessary for circular motion.</li>
<li>Define centripetal force as a force directed towards the centre of a circle.</li>
<li>Learn and apply the formula for centripetal force.</li>
<li>Explore everyday examples of centripetal force.</li>
<li>Relate the concept to vehicles, satellites, and planetary motion.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<!-- SCIENCE INVOLVED -->
<div class="card blue">
<div class="card-header">
<i class="fas fa-lightbulb"></i>
Science Involved
</div>
<div class="card-body">
<ul>
<li>Centripetal force acts towards the centre of circular motion.</li>
<li>It keeps a rotating object moving along a circular path.</li>
<li>In this exhibit, centripetal force prevents the water from spilling.</li>
<li>The inward force balances the circular motion of the water.</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>

<!-- WORKING & OBSERVATION -->
<div class="card orange">
<div class="card-header">
<i class="fas fa-cog"></i>
Working and Observation
</div>
<div class="card-body">
<ul>
<li>Fill three-fourths of the circular bowls with water.</li>
<li>Switch on the model to start rotation.</li>
<li>Observe that the bowls rotate at high speed.</li>
<li>Notice that the water does not spill.</li>
<li>The inward centripetal force keeps the water in circular motion.</li>
<li>The force acts continuously towards the centre of rotation.</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>

<!-- APPLICATIONS -->
<div class="card pink">
<div class="card-header">
<i class="fas fa-cogs"></i>
Applications
</div>
<div class="card-body">
<ul>
<li><strong>Planetary Orbits:</strong> Gravity provides the centripetal force for planets.</li>
<li><strong>Vehicle Turns:</strong> Friction between tyres and the road provides centripetal force.</li>
<li><strong>Roller Coasters:</strong> Centripetal force keeps the cars on the track.</li>
<li><strong>Satellites:</strong> Earth's gravity keeps satellites in orbit.</li>
<li><strong>Spinning Objects:</strong> String tension keeps a spinning ball moving in a circle.</li>
</ul>
</div>
