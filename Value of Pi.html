VALUE OF PI (π)

(Mathematical Constant & Circle Geometry)

Academic Information

Class: VII onwards

Topic: Value of Pi (π)

Concepts: Circles, Circumference, Diameter, Irrational Numbers

Learning Outcomes
  • Develop a comprehensive understanding of the value of π (pi).
  • Understand π as the ratio of a circle's circumference to its diameter.
  • Apply the concept of π to solve real-world problems involving circles.
  • Enhance mathematical reasoning and problem-solving skills.
Science Explained
  • Pi (π) is the ratio of the circumference of any circle to its diameter.
  • It is a mathematical constant.
  • The value of π remains the same regardless of the size of the circle.
  • It is an irrational number with an infinite, non-repeating decimal expansion.

π = Circumference ÷ Diameter

The ratio remains constant for every circle ≈ 3.14159
Working and Observation
  • Measure the diameter of a circle.
  • Measure its circumference.
  • Divide the circumference by the diameter.
  • Observe that the result is approximately 3.14159.
  • Repeat the activity with circles of different sizes.
  • Notice that the ratio remains constant for every circle.
  • The symbol π is pronounced "pie" and is the 16th letter of the Greek alphabet.

Although π is irrational, 22/7 is widely used for practical calculations and estimations.

Applications
  • Geometry: Used to calculate the circumference, area, surface area, and volume of various shapes.
  • Engineering and Design: Helps design precise machinery and solve engineering problems.
  • Computing Technology: Used in computer algorithms and simulations.
  • Physics and Science: Appears in equations involving waves, oscillations, and many physical phenomena.
  • Daily Life: Supports technological development, engineering applications, and scientific research across multiple disciplines.
