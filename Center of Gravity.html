CENTER OF GRAVITY

(Stability, Balance & Equilibrium)

Academic Information

Class: IX & XI

Topic: Centre of Gravity (CG)

Concepts: Stability, Equilibrium, Friction

Learning Outcomes
  • Investigate the concept of the Centre of Gravity (CG).
  • Understand how the centre of gravity affects the stability of objects.
  • Apply this knowledge to problems related to balance and equilibrium.
Principle
  • Every object has a Centre of Gravity (CG), the point where its weight appears to be concentrated.
  • The cone and cylinder have different shapes and therefore different stability characteristics.
  • Their motion on the zigzag path is influenced by their centre of gravity, friction, and gravitational force.
  • The geometric shape of an object affects how it responds to changes in motion.
Working
  • A cone and a cylinder move along a zigzag path.
  • The cone's pointed shape causes its centre of gravity to shift during motion.
  • This affects its stability and direction of movement.
  • The cylinder's uniform shape provides more predictable motion.
  • Friction between the objects and the path affects their speed and movement.
  • The zigzag path introduces changes in direction and elevation, influencing the motion of both objects.
Participate & Observe
  • Release the cone and cylinder from the same point.
  • Observe their movement along the zigzag path.
  • Compare the stability of the cone and cylinder.
  • Notice how shape affects motion and balance.
  • Identify the role of friction and gravity in their movement.
Applications
  • Helps understand balance and stability in everyday objects.
  • Used in engineering and design to improve the stability of structures and vehicles.
  • Demonstrates important concepts related to motion, equilibrium, and centre of gravity.
  • Provides a practical understanding of how shape influences movement.
