MARS

(The Red Planet)

Academic Information

Class: IV onwards

Topic: Mars

Concepts: Martian Geology, Atmosphere, Rovers & Exploration

Learning Outcomes
  • Understand the physical characteristics and geological features of Mars.
  • Learn about Mars's rotation and orbit around the Sun.
  • Explore the history of Mars's discovery and observation.
  • Gain insights into scientific research and future exploration of Mars.
Science Explained

🌋 Physical Characteristics

  • Volcanoes: Home to Olympus Mons, the largest volcano in the Solar System.
  • Valleys: Features Valles Marineris, one of the largest canyon systems known.
  • Impact Craters: Numerous craters indicate past collisions with asteroids and comets.
  • Atmosphere: Composed mainly of carbon dioxide, with traces of nitrogen and argon.
  • Temperature: Thin atmosphere with an average temperature of about -60°C.

🌀 Rotational and Orbital Properties

  • Rotation: Mars completes one rotation in approximately 24.6 hours (one Martian day or sol).
  • Day and Night: Rotation causes regular cycles of day and night, similar to Earth.
  • Orbit: Mars revolves around the Sun beyond Earth's orbit.

🔭 Discovery

  • Ancient Observations: Known since ancient times due to its reddish appearance in the night sky.
  • Telescopic Observations: Early astronomers, including Galileo Galilei, studied Mars and observed its surface features.
Working and Observation
  • Observe Mars as the "Red Planet" due to iron-rich dust on its surface.
  • Study models and images showing volcanoes, valleys, and impact craters.
  • Compare the Martian day with Earth's day.
  • Examine the thin atmosphere and cold environmental conditions.
  • Analyze images and data collected from orbiters and rovers.
Participate & Observe
  • Identify Mars in the Solar System model.
  • Observe the surface features such as Olympus Mons and Valles Marineris.
  • Compare Mars's atmosphere and temperature with Earth's.
  • Discuss evidence suggesting the presence of water in Mars's past.
  • Explore rover images showing the Martian landscape.
Applications

🛰️ Observation and Exploration

  • Space Missions: Numerous orbiters, landers, and rovers have explored Mars.
  • Mars Rovers: Spirit, Opportunity, Curiosity, and Perseverance have studied Martian rocks, soil, and atmosphere.

🔬 Scientific Applications

  • Helps scientists study planetary geology and climate evolution.
  • Supports research on the possibility of past or present life on Mars.
  • Provides insights into the habitability of other planets.
  • Comparison with Earth and Venus improves understanding of planetary evolution.
  • Assists in planning future human exploration and settlement missions.
