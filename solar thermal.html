SOLAR THERMAL POWER

(Minerals and Energy Resources)

Academic Information

Class: 10

Book Name: Contemporary India – II

Subject: Geography

Chapter: Minerals and Energy Resources

Learning Objectives
  • Learn about solar thermal power plants.
  • Understand how sunlight is converted into thermal energy.
  • Study the process of electricity generation using solar heat.
  • Explore different types of solar thermal systems.
  • Understand their efficiency and applications.
  • Recognize their potential in the global energy landscape.
Principle
  • Solar thermal plants use mirrors or lenses to concentrate sunlight.
  • Concentrated sunlight heats a working fluid.
  • The heated fluid produces high-temperature steam.
  • Steam drives turbines to generate electricity.
  • The system converts solar energy into thermal and electrical energy.
Working
  • Mirrors concentrate sunlight onto a receiver.
  • The receiver heats a working fluid.
  • The heated fluid produces steam.
  • Steam rotates the turbine.
  • The turbine drives the generator.
  • The generator produces electricity.
Applications
  • Large-scale electricity generation in solar thermal power plants, providing renewable and low-carbon energy.
  • Heating applications in residential, commercial, and industrial settings, including water heating and space heating.
  • Industrial processes that require thermal energy, such as material processing and drying.
