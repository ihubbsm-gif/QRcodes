RADIO TELESCOPE

(Lifelines of National Economy)

Academic Information

Class: 10

Book Name: Contemporary India – II

Chapter: Lifelines of National Economy

Learning Objectives
  • Understand the concept of a radio telescope.
  • Learn how radio waves are used to study celestial objects.
  • Know the importance of radio telescopes in space research and astronomy.
Principle
  • A radio telescope uses a large dish-shaped antenna to collect radio waves from space.
  • The collected radio waves are converted into electrical signals for analysis.
  • These signals help scientists study objects that cannot be observed clearly with visible light.
Working
  • The parabolic dish collects radio waves from stars, planets, and galaxies.
  • The dish reflects the radio waves to a receiver placed at its focus.
  • The receiver converts the radio waves into electrical signals.
  • Computers process these signals to produce images and scientific data.
Applications
  • Studying stars, planets, galaxies, and other celestial objects.
  • Detecting radio signals from distant regions of the universe.
  • Tracking satellites and space probes.
  • Supporting space exploration and astronomical research.
  • Observing objects hidden by dust or invisible to optical telescopes.
