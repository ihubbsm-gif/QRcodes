FAST BREEDER NUCLEAR POWER REACTOR

(Minerals and Energy Resources)

Academic Information

Class: 10

Book Name: Contemporary India – II

Chapter: Minerals and Energy Resources

Introduction
  • Fast breeder nuclear power reactors represent a promising advancement in nuclear energy technology.
  • They offer increased fuel efficiency and reduced nuclear waste.
  • Operating on the principles of nuclear fission and breeding, these reactors help produce sustainable and reliable energy.
Learning Objectives
  • Understand the contribution of atomic energy to development and energy security.
  • Explain the basic principles of nuclear energy.
  • Understand the operation and significance of fast breeder reactors.
  • Discuss their advantages in energy production and sustainability.
Principle Behind Exhibit
  • Fast breeder reactors operate on the principle of nuclear fission and fuel breeding.
  • They convert fertile material such as Uranium-238 (U238) into fissile material such as Plutonium-239 (Pu239) through neutron capture.
  • Unlike conventional reactors, fast breeder reactors use fast neutrons, allowing more efficient production of nuclear fuel.
Theory Behind the Reactor
  • Uranium Composition: Natural uranium contains about 99.3% U238 and 0.7% U235.
  • Fission Process: U235 undergoes fission and releases large amounts of energy.
  • Breeder Process: U238 absorbs fast neutrons and is converted into Pu239.
  • Fuel Composition: A mixture of plutonium and uranium produces more energy than natural uranium alone.
  • The Prototype Fast Breeder Reactor (PFBR) at Kalpakkam, Tamil Nadu, uses plutonium-uranium oxide fuel and liquid sodium as coolant.
Working
  • Fast neutrons initiate fission reactions inside the reactor core.
  • During the process, Uranium-238 captures neutrons and gradually transforms into Plutonium-239.
  • The generated heat is used to produce steam.
  • Steam rotates turbines connected to generators, producing electricity.
  • The newly produced plutonium can be reused as fuel, creating a self-sustaining nuclear fuel cycle.
Participate & Observe
  • Observe the reactor model and identify its major components.
  • Trace the conversion of Uranium-238 into Plutonium-239.
  • Understand how nuclear fission produces heat energy.
  • Discuss how fast breeder reactors improve fuel utilization.
  • Compare fast breeder reactors with conventional nuclear reactors.
Applications
  • Electricity generation for homes, industries, and infrastructure.
  • Improved utilization of available nuclear fuel resources.
  • Reduction in long-term nuclear waste generation.
  • Support for sustainable and long-term energy production.
  • Enhancement of national energy security.
Significance
  • Energy Security: Provides a reliable and economically viable source of power.
  • Nuclear Fuel Cycle: Makes efficient use of nuclear materials for sustained energy generation.
  • Resource Conservation: Extends the availability of nuclear fuel resources.
  • Environmental Benefits: Helps reduce nuclear waste and supports cleaner energy production.
Learning Outcome
  • Understand the principles and operation of fast breeder nuclear reactors.
  • Explain the process of nuclear fission and fuel breeding.
  • Recognize the importance of fast breeder reactors in energy security and sustainability.
  • Appreciate the role of advanced nuclear technology in meeting future energy demands.
