Learning Objectives
  • Understand the contribution of atomic energy to peace, health, and prosperity.
  • Explain nuclear energy's basic principles.
Principle Behind Exhibit
  • The Prototype Fast Breeder Reactor (PFBR) at Kalpakkam near Chennai uses plutonium-uranium oxide as fuel and liquid sodium as coolant.
Working
  • Uranium fuel is placed inside the reactor core.
  • Neutrons split uranium atoms in a fission reaction.
  • The reaction releases a large amount of heat.
  • Liquid sodium carries the heat away from the reactor.
  • The heat produces steam to drive turbines.
  • The turbines generate electricity.
  • The reactor also converts U-238 into Pu-239 for future use.
Theory Behind the Reactor
  • Uranium Composition: Contains U-238 and 0.7% U-235.
  • Fission Process: U-235 can be split using slow neutrons to generate heat.
  • Breeder Reactors: Convert U-238 to Pu-239 under fast neutrons, producing power.
  • Fuel Composition: Mixing plutonium with U-238 yields more power than natural uranium reactors.
Significance
  • Energy Security: Offers economically viable technology for power production.
  • Nuclear Fuel Cycle: Efficiently uses nuclear materials for sustained energy generation.
