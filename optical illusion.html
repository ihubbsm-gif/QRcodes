OPTICAL ILLUSION

(Persistence of Vision & Visual Perception)

Academic Information

Class: VIII onwards

Topic: Optical Illusions & Visual Perception

Concepts: Persistence of Vision, Motion Pictures, Brain Interpretation

Learning Outcomes
  • Understand how optical illusions demonstrate the brain's role in interpreting sensory information.
  • Analyze the factors that create optical illusions and influence visual perception.
  • Develop critical thinking and observation skills by exploring how the brain processes visual stimuli.
  • Understand the concept of persistence of vision and its role in motion pictures and animation.
  • Appreciate the complexity of human perception and visual cognition.
Science Involved
  • Persistence of Vision is a phenomenon in which our eyes and brain retain an image for a short period after it disappears.
  • Usually, the image of an object remains on the retina for approximately 1/16th of a second. This duration is known as persistence of vision.
  • The human eye can process only a limited number of images per second, typically around 10 to 12 images.
  • When a series of images is presented at a faster rate, the brain blends them together, creating the illusion of continuous motion.
Working and Observation

The exhibit consists of three models demonstrating different optical illusion effects:

1️⃣ Rotating Slanting Lines

  • The first model displays slanting lines on a rotating disc.
  • As the disc rotates, the lines appear to form concentric circles around a central point.

2️⃣ Coloured Circles in Motion

  • The second model consists of two coloured circles intersecting each other on a rotating disc.
  • As the disc rotates, the circles appear to move around the smaller central circle, creating an illusion of motion.

3️⃣ Hidden Circular Design

  • The third model displays a circular pattern.
  • As the disc rotates, the lines forming the design become difficult to distinguish, creating a unique visual illusion.
Participate & Observe
  • Observe each rotating disc carefully.
  • Compare what is actually present on the disc with what appears to be happening.
  • Identify how movement creates visual illusions.
  • Discuss how the brain interprets rapidly changing images.
  • Relate the observations to the concept of persistence of vision.
Applications

🎭 1. Creating 3D Effects

  • Optical illusions can make two-dimensional objects appear three-dimensional.
  • Artists and designers use these techniques to create depth and realism.

🎬 2. Motion Pictures

  • The principle of persistence of vision forms the basis of movies and video displays.
  • Rapidly displayed images create the illusion of continuous motion.

🎨 3. Animation and Visual Media

  • Animators use a sequence of still images to create moving characters and scenes.
  • This principle is widely used in films, cartoons, and digital media.
Learning Outcome
  • Explain the concept of persistence of vision.
  • Understand how optical illusions influence visual perception.
  • Relate optical illusion principles to motion pictures and animation.
  • Analyze how the brain interprets visual information to create perceived motion and depth.
