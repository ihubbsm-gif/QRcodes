HUBBLE TELESCOPE & INTERNATIONAL SPACE STATION (ISS)

(Space Technology & Exploration)

Academic Information

Class: VIII onwards

Topic: Hubble Telescope & ISS

Concepts: Space Observatories, Microgravity Research, International Collaboration

Learning Outcomes
  • Understand the purpose, design, and functions of the Hubble Space Telescope and the International Space Station (ISS).
  • Learn about the scientific achievements and discoveries made possible by Hubble and the ISS.
  • Appreciate the role of international collaboration in space exploration and research.
  • Develop an understanding of modern space technologies and scientific investigations.
Hubble Space Telescope (HST)

🎯 Purpose

  • Launched in 1990.
  • Designed to observe distant stars, galaxies, and other celestial objects.
  • Operates in visible, ultraviolet, and near-infrared wavelengths.

🔧 Design

  • A large space-based observatory.
  • Equipped with a 2.4-meter primary mirror.
  • Contains cameras, spectrographs, sensors, and other scientific instruments.

🏆 Achievements

  • Captured deep-field images of the universe.
  • Contributed to the discovery and study of exoplanets.
  • Helped measure the expansion rate of the universe.
  • Observed black holes, nebulae, and other celestial phenomena.
International Space Station (ISS)

🎯 Purpose

  • A habitable artificial satellite and microgravity laboratory.
  • Supports scientific research across multiple disciplines.

🔧 Design

  • A modular structure consisting of pressurized modules, solar arrays, and docking ports.
  • Orbits Earth approximately every 90 minutes.
  • Maintains an altitude of about 400 km above Earth.

🤝 Collaborators

  • Built and operated through international cooperation involving NASA, Roscosmos, JAXA, ESA, and CSA.
Working and Observations

🔭 Hubble Space Telescope

Working

  • Operates outside Earth's atmosphere.
  • Avoids atmospheric distortion and captures clearer images.
  • Orbits Earth approximately every 97 minutes.
  • Collects scientific data and transmits it to Earth for analysis.

Observations

  • Observes distant galaxies, nebulae, stars, and planets.
  • Provides valuable information about the structure and evolution of the universe.
  • Has contributed to numerous scientific discoveries and breakthroughs.

🛰️ International Space Station (ISS)

Working

  • Supports long-duration missions in space.
  • Houses astronauts and cosmonauts in a microgravity environment.
  • Facilitates experiments in biology, physics, astronomy, and Earth sciences.

Observations

  • Provides a unique platform for Earth observation.
  • Studies climate patterns, natural disasters, and atmospheric phenomena.
  • Conducts experiments that require microgravity conditions.
Applications

🔭 Hubble Space Telescope

  • Enhances understanding of the universe's structure, formation, and evolution.
  • Supports the study of planets beyond our solar system.
  • Contributes to the search for potentially habitable worlds.
  • Inspires interest in science and astronomy through educational outreach and imagery.

🛰️ International Space Station (ISS)

  • Advances scientific knowledge through microgravity research.
  • Tests new technologies and materials for future space missions.
  • Demonstrates the benefits of international cooperation in science and exploration.
  • Studies the effects of long-term spaceflight on the human body.
  • Provides valuable insights for future missions to Mars and beyond.
