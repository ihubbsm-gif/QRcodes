WORKING OF IRIS IN OUR EYES

(Pupil Dilation & Light Regulation)

Academic Information

Class: VIII onwards

Topic: Working of the Iris

Concepts: Pupil Dilation, Light Intensity, Eye Anatomy

Learning Outcomes
  • Analyze the operational principles of the iris.
  • Understand the role of the iris in controlling pupil size.
  • Evaluate the relationship between pupil size and light intensity.
  • Explain how the iris regulates the amount of light entering the eye.
  • Draw or create a 2D representation showing pupil changes during day and night.
Science Explained
  • The iris controls the size of the pupil in response to light.
  • It regulates the amount of light entering the eye.
  • In bright light, the pupil becomes smaller to reduce light intake.
  • In dim light, the pupil becomes larger to allow more light to enter.
  • This automatic adjustment helps the eye adapt to different lighting conditions.
Working and Observation

Observe how the iris changes the pupil's diameter based on surrounding light conditions.

🌙 Low-Light Conditions

  • The iris muscles relax.
  • The pupil dilates (enlarges).
  • More light enters the eye.

☀️ Bright-Light Conditions

  • The iris muscles contract.
  • The pupil constricts (becomes smaller).
  • Less light enters the eye.

The model demonstrates pupil contraction and dilation under different light intensities. Students can observe this effect in real time by looking at their own pupils in a mirror under different lighting conditions.

Applications
  • Enables clear vision in both bright and dim lighting conditions.
  • Helps the eyes automatically regulate light intake.
  • Supports comfortable and effective vision during both daytime and nighttime.
