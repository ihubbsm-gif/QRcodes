<!-- PRINCIPLE (Science Involved) -->
<div class="card blue">
<div class="card-header">
<i class="fas fa-lightbulb"></i>
Principle
</div>
<div class="card-body">
<ul>
<li>The principle behind the motion of a circular wooden block hanging by ropes involves the interplay of mass, inertia, and gravitational force.</li>
<li>Every object possesses inertia, which is the tendency to resist changes in its state of motion.</li>
<li>The mass of an object determines the amount of inertia it possesses.</li>
<li>Gravity acts on the suspended block, influencing its motion.</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>

<!-- WORKING -->
<div class="card purple">
<div class="card-header">
<i class="fas fa-cog"></i>
Working
</div>
<div class="card-body">
<ul>
<li>A circular wooden block is suspended using ropes.</li>
<li>When the block is hanging, it experiences the force of gravity pulling it downward.</li>
<li>Due to its mass and the gravitational force acting upon it, the block possesses potential energy.</li>
<li>When released, the potential energy is converted into kinetic energy as the block moves downward.</li>
<li>As the block descends, inertia keeps it moving.</li>
<li>At its lowest point, most of the potential energy has been converted into kinetic energy.</li>
<li>The momentum gained during descent allows the block to move upward on the opposite side.</li>
<li>Gravity gradually slows the upward motion.</li>
<li>The block momentarily stops at its highest point before reversing direction and repeating the motion.</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>

<!-- PARTICIPATE & OBSERVE -->
<div class="card purple full-width">
<div class="card-header">
<i class="fas fa-hand-pointer"></i>
Participate &amp; Observe
</div>
<div class="card-body">
<ul>
<li>Pull the wooden block to one side and release it.</li>
<li>Observe the downward and upward movement of the block.</li>
<li>Identify the lowest and highest points of motion.</li>
<li>Notice how the block continues moving due to inertia.</li>
<li>Observe the conversion of potential energy into kinetic energy and back again.</li>
<li>Compare the motion with your understanding of gravity and momentum.</li>
<li>Discuss how mass affects the motion of the suspended block.</li>
</ul>
</div>
