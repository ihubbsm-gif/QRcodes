<!-- PRINCIPLE (Science Involved) -->
<div class="card blue">
<div class="card-header">
<i class="fas fa-lightbulb"></i>
Principle
</div>
<div class="card-body">
<ul>
<li>A serpentine pendulum consists of multiple pendulums with varying thread lengths.</li>
<li>Each pendulum acts as an independent oscillator and exhibits simple harmonic motion due to gravity.</li>
<li>When several pendulums are placed together, their motions influence one another through coupled oscillations.</li>
<li>The interaction between pendulums creates wave-like motion and interesting visual patterns.</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>

<!-- WORKING -->
<div class="card purple">
<div class="card-header">
<i class="fas fa-cog"></i>
Working
</div>
<div class="card-body">
<ul>
<li>Each pendulum has a different length and therefore a different natural frequency of oscillation.</li>
<li>As the pendulums swing, the motion of one pendulum affects the neighboring pendulums.</li>
<li>Energy is transferred between the pendulums through their coupled motion.</li>
<li>Because the pendulums oscillate at different frequencies, a complex wavy or serpentine pattern is formed.</li>
<li>This continuous interaction creates the appearance of a travelling wave.</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>

<div class="card pink full-width">

<li>Used as an educational model to visualize complex wave phenomena.</li>

</ul>
