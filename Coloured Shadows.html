COLOURED SHADOWS

(Light, Colour Mixing & Wavelength)

Academic Information

Class: VI to X

Subject: Science

Concepts: Light Propagation, Reflection, Colour Mixing

Learning Outcome
  • Explore how coloured shadows are formed due to the interaction of light with different surfaces.
  • Analyze the principles of colour mixing and light behaviour in creating these effects.
  • Observe how different coloured light sources affect the colour and appearance of shadows.
  • Understand the role of light propagation, reflection, and absorption in shadow formation.
Principle
  • Shadows are formed when an object blocks light, creating an area of darkness behind it.
  • The formation of shadows depends on the principles of light propagation.
  • When light encounters an object, it can be absorbed, transmitted, or reflected.
  • In the case of shadows, the object absorbs or reflects light, preventing it from reaching the surface behind it.
  • The size and shape of the shadow depend on the intensity of the light source, direction of the light source, and distance between the object and the surface where the shadow is cast.
Working
  • Multiple light sources emitting different coloured lights are used.
  • Each colour of light has a different wavelength and interacts differently with objects. For example, red light has a longer wavelength than blue light.
  • When objects are placed in the path of these coloured lights, they block certain colours of light.
  • As a result, shadows of different colours are formed depending on the wavelength of the blocked light.
  • The experiment demonstrates how the colour composition of shadows changes based on the colour of the incident light.
  • It helps students understand colour mixing and the behaviour of light.
Participate & Observe
  • Place an object in front of red, green, and blue light sources.
  • Observe the formation of coloured shadows on a white screen.
  • Compare how the shadow colour changes when one light source is turned off.
  • Note the relationship between the blocked light and the colour of the shadow formed.
  • Observe how shadow size and colour intensity change with distance and light position.
Application
  • Artists and photographers use the interaction of light and shadow to create depth and visual effects.
  • Astronomers study shadows to understand the geometry and movement of celestial bodies.
  • Engineers and architects use shadow studies to improve natural lighting and reduce energy consumption in buildings.
  • Used in stage lighting, visual effects, and scientific demonstrations involving colour and light.
  • Helps in understanding the behaviour of light in everyday life and various scientific fields.
