MODERN GAS TURBINE

(Minerals and Energy Resources)

Academic Information

Class: 10

Book Name: Contemporary India – II

Subject: Geography

Chapter: Minerals and Energy Resources

Learning Objectives
  • Learn about the operation of modern gas turbines.
  • Understand the main components of gas turbines.
  • Study their role in electricity generation.
  • Understand their efficiency in power plants.
  • Explore their contribution to reducing greenhouse gas emissions.
  • Compare them with traditional coal-fired power plants.
Principle
  • Gas turbines operate on the Brayton cycle.
  • Air is compressed before combustion.
  • Compressed air is mixed with fuel.
  • The fuel-air mixture is ignited.
  • High-temperature, high-pressure gases are produced.
  • Expanding gases rotate the turbine.
  • The turbine drives an electric generator.
Working
  • Air is drawn into the compressor.
  • The air is compressed to high pressure.
  • Fuel is mixed with the compressed air.
  • The mixture is ignited in the combustion chamber.
  • Hot gases expand and rotate the turbine.
  • The turbine drives the generator.
  • Electricity is produced and supplied to the grid.
Applications
  • Used in electricity generation.
  • Widely used in combined cycle power plants.
  • Used in aircraft engines.
  • Used for marine propulsion.
  • Used as backup power sources.
  • Improves efficiency in modern power systems.
