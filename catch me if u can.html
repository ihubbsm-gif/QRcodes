CATCH ME IF YOU CAN

(Light-Dependent Resistor & Electromagnet)

Academic Information

Class: VII onwards

Topic: Light-Dependent Resistors (LDR)

Concepts: LDR Sensors, Circuits, Relays & Electromagnets

Learning Outcomes
  • Develop an understanding of Light-Dependent Resistors (LDRs).
  • Understand the role of LDR sensors in activating circuits and relays.
  • Explain the working principle of an LDR.
  • Learn how LDRs respond to changes in light intensity.
  • Design and implement circuits using LDR sensors.
  • Understand the use of LDRs in controlling electronic components.
Science Involved
  • A Light-Dependent Resistor (LDR) changes its resistance according to the intensity of light falling on it.
  • When light intensity changes, the electrical resistance of the LDR also changes.
  • This change in resistance is used to activate or deactivate an electrical circuit.
  • The activated circuit controls a relay and other electronic components.
  • Electromagnets can be operated automatically through this mechanism.
Working and Observation
  • Three LDRs are placed opposite three light sources.
  • The arrangement forms a perimeter around a sphere.
  • Move your hand over the setup and observe what happens.
  • The hand blocks the light reaching the LDRs.
  • The interruption of light activates the circuit and relay.
  • The relay energizes the electromagnet.
  • The electromagnet pulls the sphere downward.
  • Removing the hand restores the light path.
  • The circuit is deactivated and the sphere rises again.
Applications
  • Burglar Alarms: Detect sudden changes in light intensity and signal potential intrusions.
  • Photosensitive Relays: Control circuits based on changes in light.
  • Automatic Street Lights: Turn on at dusk and off at dawn.
  • Night Lamps: Operate automatically according to ambient light.
  • Smoke Detectors: Detect smoke through changes in light scattering.
  • Automatic Brightness & Contrast Control in Televisions: Adjust screen settings according to surrounding light conditions.
