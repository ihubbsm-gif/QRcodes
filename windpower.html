SOURCES OF POWER - WIND POWER

Academic Information

Class: 10

Subject: Geography

Book Name: Contemporary India – II

Chapter: Minerals and Energy Resources

Learning Objectives
  • Understand various sources of power used for electricity generation.
  • Differentiate between renewable and non-renewable energy resources.
  • Study the technology and working principles of modern wind turbines.
  • Explore historical uses and advancements of windmills and wind pumps.
  • Analyze environmental and economic impacts of wind energy.
Science Involved
  • Wind possesses kinetic energy due to the movement of air.
  • Wind turbines capture this kinetic energy through rotating blades.
  • The rotating blades drive a generator that converts mechanical energy into electrical energy.
  • Wind power is a renewable and clean source of energy.
  • Electricity generation from wind reduces dependence on fossil fuels and lowers carbon emissions.
Applications
  • Wind Farms: Large-scale renewable electricity generation.
  • Agriculture: Traditional windmills used for grinding grain and mechanical work.
  • Water Pumping: Wind pumps supply water for irrigation and rural communities.
  • Sustainable Development: Clean energy production with minimal environmental impact.
  • Rural Electrification: Providing electricity to remote areas.
