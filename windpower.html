SOURCES OF POWER

(Wind Mill)

Academic Information

Class: 10

Book Name: Contemporary India – II

Chapter: Minerals and Energy Resources

Learning Objectives
  • Understand the various sources of power used for generating electricity, including renewable and non-renewable energy sources.
  • Explore the basic principles and working mechanisms behind different methods of power generation.
  • Examine the applications of various power sources in domestic, industrial, and commercial sectors.
  • Analyze the environmental and economic impacts of different energy resources and their role in sustainable development.
Science Involved
  • Wind contains kinetic energy.
  • Wind turns the turbine blades.
  • The rotating blades produce mechanical energy.
  • A generator converts it into electricity.
  • Wind power is a clean and renewable source of energy.
Participate & Observe
  • Observe the rotation of a windmill or wind turbine in moving air.
  • Notice how faster wind causes the blades to rotate more quickly.
  • Identify the main parts such as blades, tower, and generator.
  • Discuss how wind energy is converted into electricity without causing pollution.
Applications
  • Electricity Generation: Wind farms produce clean and renewable electricity.
  • Agriculture: Windmills provide mechanical power for farming activities.
  • Water Pumping: Wind pumps lift water for irrigation and rural use.
  • Sustainable Farming: Wind energy supports eco-friendly agricultural practices.
  • Rural Development: Wind power helps provide energy in remote areas.
