</div>
<div class="card-body">
<ul>
<li>The ear is a complex sensory organ responsible for hearing and balance.</li>
<li>Located on either side of the head.</li>
<li>It converts sound waves into electrical signals and helps maintain equilibrium.</li>
</ul>

<!-- STRUCTURE -->
<div class="card purple full-width">
<div class="card-header">
<i class="fas fa-shapes"></i>
Structure
</div>
<div class="card-body">

<div class="part-box">
<h4>👂 Outer Ear</h4>
<ul>
<li>Includes the pinna (auricle) and the external auditory canal.</li>
<li>The pinna collects sound waves and directs them into the canal.</li>
<li>The canal leads to the eardrum (tympanic membrane).</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="part-box">
<h4>🦴 Middle Ear</h4>
<ul>
<li>Contains three tiny bones called ossicles: Malleus (Hammer), Incus (Anvil), Stapes (Stirrup).</li>
<li>These bones amplify and transmit vibrations from the eardrum to the oval window of the inner ear.</li>
<li>The Eustachian tube connects the middle ear to the throat and helps equalize pressure.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="part-box">
<h4>🌀 Inner Ear</h4>
<ul>
<li>Comprises the cochlea and the vestibular system.</li>
<li>The cochlea contains hair cells that convert sound vibrations into electrical signals.</li>
<li>The vestibular system, including the semicircular canals and vestibule, helps maintain balance and spatial orientation.</li>
</ul>
</div>

</div>
</div>

<!-- FUNCTION OF EACH PART -->
<div class="card orange full-width">
<div class="card-header">
<i class="fas fa-cog"></i>
Function of Each Part
</div>
<div class="card-body">

<div class="func-box">
<h4>👂 Pinna (Auricle)</h4>
<ul>
<li>Collects sound waves from the surroundings.</li>
<li>Directs sound waves into the external auditory canal.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="func-box">
<h4>🎚️ External Auditory Canal</h4>
<ul>
<li>Carries sound waves to the eardrum.</li>
<li>Protects the inner parts of the ear.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="func-box">
<h4>🥁 Eardrum (Tympanic Membrane)</h4>
<ul>
<li>Vibrates when sound waves strike it.</li>
<li>Transfers vibrations to the ossicles.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="func-box">
<h4>🔨 Malleus (Hammer)</h4>
<ul>
<li>Receives vibrations from the eardrum.</li>
<li>Passes vibrations to the incus.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="func-box">
<h4>⚒️ Incus (Anvil)</h4>
<ul>
<li>Transmits vibrations from the malleus to the stapes.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="func-box">
<h4>🦶 Stapes (Stirrup)</h4>
<ul>
<li>Amplifies and transmits vibrations to the inner ear through the oval window.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="func-box">
<h4>🎈 Eustachian Tube</h4>
<ul>
<li>Equalizes air pressure on both sides of the eardrum.</li>
<li>Connects the middle ear to the throat.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="func-box">
<h4>🐚 Cochlea</h4>
<ul>
<li>Converts sound vibrations into electrical signals using specialized hair cells.</li>
<li>Sends these signals to the brain for interpretation.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="func-box">
<h4>⚖️ Vestibular System</h4>
<ul>
<li>Includes the semicircular canals and vestibule.</li>
<li>Detects head movement and body position.</li>
<li>Maintains balance and spatial orientation.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="func-box">
<h4>🧠 Auditory Nerve</h4>
<ul>
<li>Carries electrical signals from the cochlea to the brain.</li>
<li>Enables the brain to recognize and interpret sounds.</li>
</ul>
</div>
