Science Explained
</div>
<div class="card-body">

<div class="sub-box">
<h4>🪐 Physical Characteristics</h4>
<ul>
<li><strong>Surface:</strong> Covered with numerous impact craters due to the absence of a significant atmosphere.</li>
<li><strong>Density:</strong> One of the densest planets in the Solar System, indicating a large metallic core.</li>
<li><strong>Temperature:</strong> Experiences extreme temperature variations, ranging from -173°C at night to 427°C during the day.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="sub-box">
<h4>🌀 Orbital and Rotational Properties</h4>
<ul>
<li><strong>Orbit:</strong> Closest planet to the Sun, with an elliptical orbit varying between 46 million km and 70 million km from the Sun.</li>
<li><strong>Rotation:</strong> Exhibits a 3:2 spin-orbit resonance, rotating three times for every two revolutions around the Sun.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="sub-box">
<h4>🔭 Discovery</h4>
<ul>
<li>Known to ancient civilizations because it is visible to the naked eye.</li>
<li>Early telescopic observations by Galileo Galilei provided more detailed information about the planet.</li>
</ul>
</div>
