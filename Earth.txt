<div class="card blue full-width">
<div class="card-header">

Science Explained
</div>
<div class="card-body">

<div class="sub-box">
<h4>🌍 Physical Characteristics</h4>
<ul>
<li><strong>Layers:</strong> Earth consists of the inner core, outer core, mantle, and crust.</li>
<li><strong>Atmosphere:</strong> Composed mainly of nitrogen (78%) and oxygen (21%), along with traces of other gases.</li>
<li><strong>Hydrosphere:</strong> About 71% of Earth's surface is covered by oceans, rivers, lakes, glaciers, and ice caps.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="sub-box">
<h4>🏔️ Surface Features</h4>
<ul>
<li><strong>Continents:</strong> Earth has seven major continents and large ocean basins.</li>
<li><strong>Mountains:</strong> Includes major mountain ranges such as the Himalayas, Andes, and Rockies.</li>
<li><strong>Plate Tectonics:</strong> Movement of tectonic plates causes earthquakes, volcanic activity, and mountain formation.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="sub-box">
<h4>🌀 Rotational Properties</h4>
<ul>
<li><strong>Rotation:</strong> Earth rotates counterclockwise on its axis.</li>
<li><strong>Day and Night:</strong> One complete rotation takes approximately 24 hours, causing the cycle of day and night.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="sub-box">
<h4>🔭 Discovery</h4>
<ul>
<li><strong>Early Knowledge:</strong> Ancient civilizations studied the Sun, Moon, stars, and seasons for navigation and calendar-making.</li>
<li><strong>Scientific Revolution:</strong> Advances in astronomy, physics, and mathematics helped explain Earth's place in the Solar System.</li>
</ul>
</div>
