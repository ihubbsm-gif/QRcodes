Learning Objectives
  • Gain insights into the principles of MHD power generation.
  • Understand its working mechanism.
  • Learn about its advantages.
  • Explore its potential as a cleaner power generation technology.
  • Compare it with conventional power plants.
Principle
  • MHD stands for Magneto Hydro Dynamic power generation.
  • It uses an electrically conducting fluid, such as plasma.
  • The conducting fluid moves through a magnetic field.
  • The interaction induces an electric current.
  • Electricity is generated without conventional rotating turbines.
Working
  • Fuel is heated to produce high-temperature plasma.
  • The plasma flows through a strong magnetic field.
  • The moving plasma induces an electric current.
  • Electrodes collect the generated electricity.
  • The electricity is supplied to the power system.
  • The exhaust heat can be reused to improve efficiency.
Applications
  • Used in experimental power plants.
  • Improves power generation efficiency.
  • Reduces emissions compared to conventional plants.
  • Has potential for large-scale electricity generation.
  • Can supplement conventional thermal power plants.
