EYE

(Anatomy, Vision & Sensory Perception)

Academic Information

Class: VII onwards

Topic: The Human Eye

Concepts: Anatomy, Vision, Sensory Perception

About the Eye
  • The eyes are sophisticated sensory organs crucial for vision, enabling the perception of light, color, and movement.
  • Located in the eye sockets, or orbits.
  • They work in conjunction with the brain to convert visual stimuli into comprehensible images.
Learning Outcomes
  • Visual Health Awareness: Understanding eye anatomy and function is essential for recognizing and addressing vision problems such as refractive errors, cataracts, and retinal disorders.
  • Educational Value: Learning about the complex mechanisms of vision illustrates the intricate interaction between anatomical structures and sensory perception, highlighting the importance of maintaining eye health for overall well-being.
Structure

👁️ Cornea

  • The transparent, dome-shaped front of the eye.
  • Helps focus light onto the retina.

🔍 Lens

  • Located behind the iris.
  • Adjusts its shape to fine-tune the focus of light onto the retina.

🟤 Iris

  • The colored part of the eye.
  • Controls the size of the pupil.
  • Regulates the amount of light entering the eye.

⚫ Pupil

  • The black, central opening in the iris.
  • Changes size to control light intake.

🟥 Retina

  • The light-sensitive layer at the back of the eye.
  • Contains photoreceptor cells (rods and cones).
  • Responsible for detecting light and color.

🧠 Optic Nerve

  • Transmits visual information from the retina to the brain.
