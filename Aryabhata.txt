Science Explained
</div>
<div class="card-body">

<div class="sub-box">
<h4>🛰️ Aryabhata Satellite</h4>
<ul>
<li>Aryabhata was India's first satellite, launched on 19 April 1975.</li>
<li>It was named after the ancient Indian mathematician and astronomer Aryabhata.</li>
<li>The satellite was designed to conduct experiments in X-ray Astronomy, Solar Physics, and Aeronomy (study of the upper atmosphere).</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="sub-box">
<h4>🎯 Mission Objectives</h4>
<ul>
<li>Study conditions in Earth's ionosphere.</li>
<li>Measure neutrons and gamma rays emitted by the Sun.</li>
<li>Conduct investigations related to cosmic X-rays and solar radiation.</li>
<li>Develop indigenous satellite technology and space research capabilities.</li>
</ul>
</div>

</div>
</div>

<!-- WORKING & OBSERVATION -->
<div class="card orange full-width">
<div class="card-header">
<i class="fas fa-eye"></i>
Working and Observation
</div>
<div class="card-body">

<div class="obs-box">
<h4>⚙️ Working</h4>
<ul>
<li>Aryabhata was a 26-sided polyhedron with a diameter of about 1.4 metres.</li>
<li>Most of its outer surface was covered with solar cells to generate electrical power.</li>
<li>It was spin-stabilized and placed in a Low Earth Orbit with an apogee of 619 km and perigee of 563 km.</li>
<li>The satellite carried instruments to measure cosmic X-rays, detect solar neutrons and gamma rays, and study the Earth's ionosphere.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="obs-box">
<h4>🔭 Observation</h4>
<ul>
<li>Although a power failure stopped scientific experiments after about four days and 60 orbits, valuable data was successfully collected.</li>
<li>The satellite's systems remained operational until March 1981.</li>
<li>Aryabhata re-entered Earth's atmosphere on 10 February 1992.</li>
</ul>
</div>

</div>
</div>

Applications

<div class="card-body">
<ul>
<li>Contributed to research in X-ray astronomy, solar physics, and ionospheric studies.</li>
<li>Provided valuable experience in satellite design, operation, and space technology.</li>
<li>Laid the foundation for India's future satellite and space missions.</li>
<li>Inspired generations of Indian scientists, engineers, and students to pursue careers in science and space research.</li>
<li>Became a symbol of India's scientific progress and self-reliance in space technology.</li>
</ul>
</div>
