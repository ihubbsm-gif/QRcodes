<div class="card blue full-width">
<div class="card-header">
<i class="fas fa-lightbulb"></i>
Science Explained: Chandrayaan Missions
</div>
<div class="card-body">

<div class="sub-box">
<h4>🛰️ Chandrayaan-1 (2008)</h4>
<ul>
<li>India's first lunar mission launched by ISRO.</li>
<li>Discovered the presence of water molecules on the Moon, a landmark achievement in lunar science.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="sub-box">
<h4>🛰️ Chandrayaan-2 (2019)</h4>
<ul>
<li>Consisted of an Orbiter, Vikram Lander, and Pragyan Rover.</li>
<li>The orbiter continues to provide valuable scientific data.</li>
<li>The Vikram lander attempted a soft landing but was unsuccessful.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="sub-box">
<h4>🛰️ Chandrayaan-3 (2023)</h4>
<ul>
<li>Successfully achieved a soft landing near the Moon's south pole.</li>
<li>Demonstrated safe landing technology and rover operations on the lunar surface.</li>
<li>Made India the first country to land near the Moon's south polar region.</li>
</ul>
</div>

</div>
</div>

<!-- WORKING & OBSERVATION -->
<div class="card orange full-width">
<div class="card-header">
<i class="fas fa-eye"></i>
Working and Observation
</div>
<div class="card-body">

<div class="obs-box">
<h4>⚙️ Working</h4>
<ul>
<li>Chandrayaan-3 consists of a Propulsion Module (carries the lander and rover to lunar orbit), Vikram Lander (performs a controlled soft landing), and Pragyan Rover (explores the lunar surface and conducts scientific experiments).</li>
<li>After reaching lunar orbit, the lander separates from the propulsion module and lands on the Moon.</li>
<li>The rover is then deployed to study the surrounding area.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="obs-box">
<h4>🔬 Observation</h4>
<ul>
<li>The mission has provided important information about lunar surface composition, temperature variations near the south pole, and the presence of water ice and other volatile substances.</li>
<li>The Pragyan rover travelled across the lunar surface, captured images, and transmitted scientific data back to Earth.</li>
</ul>
</div>

</div>
</div>

Applications

<li>Supports future lunar exploration missions and the possibility of long-term human presence on the Moon.</li>
<li>Technologies developed for Chandrayaan, such as autonomous navigation, hazard detection, and precision landing systems, can be applied to future space missions.</li>
<li>Inspires students and researchers to pursue careers in science, engineering, and space technology.</li>
