<div class="card purple">
<div class="card-header">
<i class="fas fa-history"></i>
History and Concepts
</div>
<div class="card-body">
<ul>
<li><strong>Giovanni Battista Della Porta (1606):</strong> Theorized the role of steam in creating a vacuum and increasing pressure in closed containers.</li>
<li><strong>Denis Papin (1679):</strong> Turned theories into reality with the first pressure cooker and expanded the device with a sliding piston.</li>
<li><strong>Hero of Alexandria (AD 75):</strong> Presented plans for a simple steam engine, laying the foundation for later steam technology.</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>

<!-- WORKING -->
<div class="card orange">
<div class="card-header">
<i class="fas fa-cog"></i>
Working
</div>
<div class="card-body">
<ul>
<li>Water is heated to produce steam.</li>
<li>As water turns into steam, it expands and creates pressure inside a closed container.</li>
<li>The pressure generated by the steam can be used to move objects or perform mechanical work.</li>
<li>Early inventors demonstrated that steam could create motion by pushing pistons or rotating simple devices.</li>
<li>Hero of Alexandria's steam-powered device showed the potential of steam energy through rotary motion.</li>
<li>Denis Papin's experiments with steam pressure and pistons contributed to the development of later steam engines.</li>
<li>These early discoveries laid the foundation for the advanced steam engines used during the Industrial Revolution.</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>
