BODILESS HEAD

(Reflection of Light)

Academic Information
  • Class: 8
  • Subject: Science
  • Chapter No: 13
  • Chapter: Light
Learning Objectives
Understand the definitions of terms like incident ray, reflected ray, angle of incidence, angle of reflection, and normal to the surface.
Explain how reflection by plane mirrors is used in everyday objects like mirrors, kaleidoscopes, and periscopes.
Science Involved
The exhibit uses plane mirrors to create an illusion of a hollow structure, making your head appear bodiless due to the reflection of painted walls.
Participate & Observe
  1. Enter through the side gallery of the exhibit.
  2. Pop up your head from the hole provided.
  3. Your head is seen by your friends but not your body!
Applications
Optics: Mirrors are used in periscopes, kaleidoscopes, telescopes, and microscopes.
Security: Mirrors allow visibility around corners or blind spots.
Design: Mirrors create illusions of space and reflect light in architecture and interior design.
Entertainment: Mirrors create effects in stage and theater lighting.
