ASTRONAUT – RAKESH SHARMA

(India's First Astronaut)

Academic Information

Class: VI onwards

Topic: Rakesh Sharma – India's First Astronaut

Concepts: Space Missions, International Collaboration, Space History

Learning Outcomes
  • Understand the biography and achievements of Rakesh Sharma, India's first astronaut.
  • Learn about the significance of his historic space mission.
  • Gain insights into astronaut training and space travel.
  • Appreciate the role of international cooperation in space exploration.
  • Develop interest in space science and astronomy.
Who is Rakesh Sharma?
  • Rakesh Sharma is India's first astronaut.
  • He flew into space aboard Soyuz T-11 in 1984.
  • His mission was conducted under the Soviet Intercosmos Programme.
  • He became the first Indian citizen to travel to space.
Mission Highlights
  • Launched on 3 April 1984.
  • Spent nearly 8 days in space aboard the Salyut 7 Space Station.
  • Conducted scientific and medical experiments in microgravity.
  • Represented India in one of the country's earliest milestones in space exploration.
Historical Significance
  • Marked India's first human spaceflight achievement.
  • Strengthened cooperation between India and the Soviet Union.
  • Inspired future generations to pursue careers in science, technology, and space research.
Applications / Importance
  • Encourages interest in space science and exploration.
  • Highlights the importance of astronaut training and scientific research in space.
  • Demonstrates the benefits of international collaboration in space missions.
  • Inspires students to contribute to scientific and technological advancement.
  • Serves as a milestone in India's space history.
