(Conservation, Biodiversity & Sustainable Management)

Learning Objectives
  • Understand the importance of forests in environmental conservation.
  • Learn how forests help reduce climate change by absorbing carbon dioxide.
  • Understand the role of forests in protecting biodiversity.
  • Study how geographical conditions influence different types of forests.
  • Learn about sustainable forest management practices.
Key Concepts
  • Climate Change Mitigation: Forests absorb carbon dioxide and help reduce global warming.
  • Biodiversity Conservation: Forests provide habitat for a large number of plant and animal species.
  • Geographical Distribution: Different forest types grow under different climatic and soil conditions —
    • Tropical Rainforests – Warm and humid regions
    • Temperate Forests – Moderate climates
    • Boreal Forests (Taiga) – Cold regions
  • Forest Management: Sustainable forestry, afforestation, and reforestation help maintain forest resources.
Learning Outcomes
  • Explain the ecological importance of forests.
  • Identify major forest types and their geographical distribution.
  • Understand the relationship between climate, soil, and forest growth.
  • Recognize the impact of deforestation on the environment.
  • Suggest sustainable methods for forest conservation.
  • Appreciate the role of forests in maintaining environmental balance.
Real-Life Applications
  • Climate change control through carbon absorption.
  • Wildlife and biodiversity protection.
  • Sustainable use of natural resources.
  • Soil and water conservation.
  • Reforestation and afforestation programs.
