OUR GALAXY – MILKY WAY

(Structure, Formation & Evolution)

Academic Information

Class: VI onwards

Topic: The Milky Way Galaxy

Concepts: Galactic Structure, Star Formation, Black Holes

Learning Outcomes
  • Understand the structure, components, and dynamics of the Milky Way galaxy.
  • Learn about the formation and evolution of the Milky Way.
  • Develop observational astronomy skills.
  • Understand how telescopes help study our galaxy.
Science Explained

🌌 Structure of the Milky Way

  • Galactic Bulge: Dense central region containing the supermassive black hole Sagittarius A*.
  • Galactic Disk: Contains spiral arms made of stars, gas, and dust where new stars form.
  • Galactic Halo: Surrounding spherical region containing old stars, globular clusters, and dark matter.
  • Galactic Bar: Bar-shaped structure of stars crossing the galactic centre.

📏 Size and Scale

  • Approximately 100,000 light-years in diameter.
  • Contains about 100–400 billion stars.

🌠 Formation and Evolution

  • Formed about 13.6 billion years ago through the merging of gas clouds and smaller galaxies.
  • Star formation continues in the spiral arms.
  • Stars and gas orbit the galactic centre under the influence of gravity.
Working and Observations

🌀 Galactic Motion

  • Gravity controls the movement of stars, gas, and other objects within the Milky Way.
  • This gravitational interaction helps maintain the galaxy's spiral structure.

⭐ Star Formation

  • Occurs mainly in the spiral arms.
  • Gas and dust clouds collapse under gravity to form new stars.

🕳️ Central Black Hole

  • Sagittarius A* exerts a strong gravitational influence on nearby stars and gas.
  • Helps shape the motion of objects near the galactic centre.

🔭 Observational Evidence

  • Optical Telescopes: Observe visible stars and star clusters.
  • Radio Telescopes: Detect gas clouds and radio emissions.
  • Infrared Telescopes: Observe regions hidden by dust.
  • Space Telescopes: Provide detailed observations beyond Earth's atmosphere.
Applications
  • Helps scientists understand galaxy formation and evolution.
  • Supports the study of stellar life cycles.
  • Improves knowledge of black holes and dark matter.
  • Advances astronomical research and space exploration.
  • Enhances understanding of the structure of the universe.
