VANISHING IMAGE

(Light)

Academic Information

Class: 8

Book Name: Science

Chapter No: 13

Chapter: Light

Learning Objectives
  • Explain how the angle between two mirrors affects the image characteristics (e.g., upright/inverted, number of images).
  • Identify everyday uses of mirrors at specific angles (e.g., kaleidoscopes, corner reflectors).
Science Involved
  • When two mirrors are angled greater than 180 degrees, light rays reflect successively, causing divergence.
  • This results in decreasing image intensity and eventual image vanishing as the rays spread out and become less concentrated.
Participate & Observe
  • Move left and right in front of the mirrors or stand at the central footmark to observe your image vanishing due to light ray divergence caused by the mirrors' angle.
Applications
  • Optical Design: Optimizes alignment of optical components to prevent image degradation.
  • Laser Beam Delivery Systems: Manages divergence in multi-mirror systems.
  • Optical Communication: Helps design efficient optical fibers and waveguides by minimizing light signal loss.
