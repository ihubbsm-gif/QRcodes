<div class="card-body">

<div class="sub-box">
<h4>🚀 Apollo Program</h4>
<ul>
<li>The Apollo Program was conducted by NASA between 1961 and 1972.</li>
<li>Its primary goal was to land humans on the Moon and return them safely to Earth.</li>
<li>The missions provided valuable information about lunar geology, the Moon's origin and evolution, and the relationship between the Earth and the Moon.</li>
<li>Scientific experiments included analysis of lunar soil and rocks, study of seismic activity (moonquakes), and investigation of solar wind particles.</li>
</ul>
</div>

</div>

<!-- WORKING & OBSERVATION -->
<div class="card orange full-width">
<div class="card-header">
<i class="fas fa-eye"></i>
Working and Observation
</div>
<div class="card-body">

<div class="obs-box">
<h4>⚙️ Working</h4>
<ul>
<li>Apollo missions were launched using the powerful Saturn V rocket.</li>
<li>The spacecraft consisted of three main components: Command Module (CM) – carried astronauts and returned them to Earth; Service Module (SM) – provided propulsion, power, and life support; Lunar Module (LM) – landed astronauts on the Moon's surface.</li>
<li>While the Command and Service Modules remained in lunar orbit, the Lunar Module descended to the Moon.</li>
<li>Astronauts conducted experiments, collected samples, and captured photographs of the lunar surface.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="obs-box">
<h4>🔭 Observation</h4>
<ul>
<li>Apollo astronauts returned approximately 382 kilograms of lunar rocks and soil samples.</li>
<li>These samples provided important information about the Moon's composition, geological history, and formation and evolution.</li>
<li>The missions also produced detailed photographs and scientific data about the Moon's surface and environment.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<li><strong>Space Exploration:</strong> Provided the foundation for future human and robotic space missions.</li>
