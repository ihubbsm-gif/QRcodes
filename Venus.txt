<!-- SCIENCE EXPLAINED -->
<div class="card blue">
<div class="card-header">
<i class="fas fa-lightbulb"></i>
Science Explained
</div>
<div class="card-body">
<ul>
<li><strong>Atmosphere:</strong> Venus has a thick atmosphere composed mainly of carbon dioxide (CO₂), with clouds of sulfuric acid.</li>
<li><strong>Extreme Conditions:</strong> Surface temperatures average about 462°C, making Venus the hottest planet in the Solar System.</li>
<li><strong>High Pressure:</strong> Atmospheric pressure on Venus is about 92 times greater than Earth's.</li>
<li><strong>Volcanic Surface:</strong> Venus contains vast volcanic plains, shield volcanoes, and mountains such as Maxwell Montes, its highest peak.</li>
<li><strong>Impact Craters:</strong> The planet has relatively few craters because volcanic activity has resurfaced much of its surface.</li>
<li><strong>Retrograde Rotation:</strong> Venus rotates in the opposite direction to most planets, causing the Sun to appear to rise in the west and set in the east.</li>
<li><strong>Long Day:</strong> One Venusian day lasts about 243 Earth days, while one Venusian year lasts about 225 Earth days.</li>
<li><strong>Phases of Venus:</strong> Like the Moon, Venus shows different phases when viewed from Earth due to its position relative to the Sun.</li>
</ul>
</div>
