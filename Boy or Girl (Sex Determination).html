BOY OR GIRL (SEX DETERMINATION)

(Genetics & Chromosomal Inheritance)

Academic Information

Class: X onwards

Topic: Sex Determination

Concepts: Chromosomes, Genetics, Inheritance

About Sex Determination
  • Sex determination is the biological process that determines the sex of an individual, primarily driven by genetic and chromosomal mechanisms.
  • In humans, sex determination involves the interaction between genetic information and hormonal influences that lead to the development of male or female physical characteristics.
Learning Outcomes
  • Genetic Understanding: Knowledge of sex determination enhances understanding of genetic inheritance and chromosomal disorders related to sex chromosomes, such as Turner Syndrome and Klinefelter Syndrome.
  • Educational Insight: Learning about sex determination underscores the interaction between genetics and development, illustrating how genetic factors influence physiological traits and reproductive health.
Structure

🧬 Sex Chromosomes

  • Humans have 23 pairs of chromosomes, with one pair being sex chromosomes.
  • Females have two X chromosomes (XX).
  • Males have one X and one Y chromosome (XY).
  • These sex chromosomes are crucial in determining the sex of an individual.

⚪ Testes and Ovaries

  • The testes (in males) and ovaries (in females) are the primary reproductive organs that develop as a result of sex determination.
  • Testes produce male hormones (androgens) that lead to the development of male secondary sexual characteristics.
  • Ovaries produce female hormones (estrogens and progesterone) that lead to the development of female secondary sexual characteristics.
Working
  • During fertilization, the mother always contributes an X chromosome through the egg.
  • The father contributes either an X chromosome or a Y chromosome through the sperm.
  • If an X chromosome from the mother combines with a Y chromosome from the father, the child is male (XY).
  • If an X chromosome from the mother combines with an X chromosome from the father, the child is female (XX).
  • Thus, the combination of sex chromosomes determines the sex of the child.
Participate & Observe
  • Observe a sex determination chart (Punnett Square) showing the combination of X and Y chromosomes.
  • Identify the possible outcomes (XX and XY) and understand how sex is determined genetically.
  • Discuss how chromosomes inherited from both parents contribute to the characteristics of an individual.
