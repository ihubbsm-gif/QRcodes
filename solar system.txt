<!-- SCIENCE EXPLAINED -->
<div class="card blue full-width">
<div class="card-header">
<i class="fas fa-lightbulb"></i>
Science Explained
</div>
<div class="card-body">

<p>The solar system consists of the Sun, planets, dwarf planets, moons, asteroids, comets, and other celestial objects.</p>

<div class="sub-box">
<h4>☀️ The Sun</h4>
<ul>
<li>A G-type main-sequence star located at the centre of the solar system.</li>
<li>Contains more than 99% of the solar system's mass.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="sub-box">
<h4>🪐 Planets</h4>
<ul>
<li><strong>Terrestrial Planets:</strong> Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars.</li>
<li><strong>Gas Giants:</strong> Jupiter and Saturn.</li>
<li><strong>Ice Giants:</strong> Uranus and Neptune.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="sub-box">
<h4>🌑 Dwarf Planets</h4>
<ul>
<li>Pluto</li>
<li>Eris</li>
<li>Haumea</li>
<li>Makemake</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="sub-box">
<h4>🌕 Moons</h4>
<ul>
<li>Natural satellites orbiting planets.</li>
<li>Examples include Earth's Moon, Jupiter's Ganymede, and Saturn's Titan.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="sub-box">
<h4>☄️ Small Bodies</h4>
<ul>
<li>Asteroids mainly found in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter.</li>
<li>Comets originate from the Kuiper Belt and Oort Cloud.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="sub-box">
<h4>🌠 Formation and Evolution</h4>
<ul>
<li>Formed about 4.6 billion years ago from a rotating cloud of gas and dust called the solar nebula.</li>
<li>Dust and gas combined under gravity to form planetesimals.</li>
<li>Planetesimals collided and merged to form planets.</li>
<li>Gravitational interactions shaped the present structure of the solar system.</li>
</ul>
</div>

</div>
</div>

<!-- WORKING & OBSERVATIONS -->
<div class="card orange full-width">
<div class="card-header">
<i class="fas fa-eye"></i>
Working and Observations
</div>
<div class="card-body">

<div class="obs-box">
<h4>🌍 Role of Gravity</h4>
<ul>
<li>The Sun's gravity holds the solar system together.</li>
<li>It governs the motion of planets, moons, and other celestial objects.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="obs-box">
<h4>🛰️ Planetary Motion</h4>
<ul>
<li>Planets and other objects move around the Sun in elliptical orbits.</li>
<li>Their motion depends on mass and distance from the Sun.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="obs-box">
<h4>☀️ Solar Influence</h4>
<ul>
<li>The Sun emits radiation and solar wind.</li>
<li>These affect the atmospheres and surfaces of planets and other solar system bodies.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="obs-box">
<h4>🔭 Observations</h4>
<ul>
<li>Telescopes help observe planets, moons, asteroids, and comets.</li>
<li>Space missions provide detailed information about solar system objects.</li>
<li>Observations help scientists understand planetary features and movements.</li>
</ul>
</div>

</div>
