<!-- WORKING OF SOLAR PANELS -->
<div class="card purple full-width">
<div class="card-header">
<i class="fas fa-solar-panel"></i>
Working of Solar Panels (Photovoltaic Cells)
</div>
<div class="card-body">

<div class="concept-box">
<h4>🔲 Semiconductor Layers</h4>
<ul>
<li>A solar cell consists of two silicon layers.</li>
<li><strong>N-type layer:</strong> Doped with phosphorus to provide extra electrons.</li>
<li><strong>P-type layer:</strong> Doped with boron to create electron holes.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="concept-box">
<h4>⚡ Junction Formation</h4>
<ul>
<li>Sunlight creates a photovoltaic effect at the junction of the two layers.</li>
<li>This process enables the conversion of solar energy into electrical energy.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="concept-box">
<h4>🔆 Electron Liberation</h4>
<ul>
<li>Photons from sunlight free electrons from the semiconductor atoms.</li>
<li>The released electrons move towards the N-type layer.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="concept-box">
<h4>💡 Electric Current Generation</h4>
<ul>
<li>The movement of electrons creates an electric current.</li>
<li>The solar panel converts this current into usable electricity.</li>
</ul>
</div>
