Science Explained

<div class="card-body">

<div class="concept-box">
<h4>📊 Central Limit Theorem</h4>
<ul>
<li>States that the sum or average of many independent, identically distributed variables approaches a normal distribution.</li>
<li>The Galton board demonstrates this principle through the movement of beads.</li>
<li>With a large sample size, the binomial distribution converges to a normal distribution.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="concept-box">
<h4>🎲 Binomial Distribution</h4>
<ul>
<li>Each bead makes a sequence of independent decisions.</li>
<li>At every peg, the bead has an equal probability of moving left or right.</li>
<li>The path of each bead represents a binary outcome.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="concept-box">
<h4>🔔 Normal Distribution</h4>
<ul>
<li>As more beads are released, they accumulate in a bell-shaped pattern.</li>
<li>The resulting pattern is known as the normal or Gaussian distribution.</li>
<li>Most beads collect near the centre, while fewer reach the edges.</li>
</ul>
</div>

</div>
</div>

<!-- WORKING & OBSERVATION -->
<div class="card orange">
<div class="card-header">
<i class="fas fa-cog"></i>
Working and Observation
</div>
<div class="card-body">
<ul>
<li>The Galton board consists of a vertical board with staggered rows of pegs.</li>
<li>Press the knob to release the beads from the top.</li>
<li>As the beads strike the pegs, they move left, right, or continue downward.</li>
<li>Each bead follows a random path through the board.</li>
<li>Observe the beads collecting in the lower compartments.</li>
<li>Notice that most beads accumulate in the central compartments.</li>
<li>Fewer beads reach the side compartments.</li>
<li>The overall pattern forms a bell-shaped normal distribution curve.</li>
<li>This demonstrates how many random events can produce a predictable distribution.</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>
