BEFORE AND AFTER DAM

(Water Resources)

Academic Information

Class: 10

Book Name: Contemporary India – II

Subject: Geography

Chapter: Water Resources

Learning Objectives
  • Understand the concept of dams and their functions, such as water storage and power generation.
  • Explore different types of dams and the engineering principles behind them.
  • Analyze positive and negative impacts of dams on society and the environment.
  • Develop critical thinking skills by evaluating dam projects and their trade-offs.
Concept Involved
  • Dams manipulate water flow through a barrier, creating a reservoir upstream that allows for various functions.
  • Purpose: Show the benefits and physical changes of constructing a dam in a narrow part of a deep river valley.
Working
  • Water flowing through a river is blocked by a dam, creating a reservoir.
  • The stored water is collected behind the dam and regulated through gates.
  • When released, the flowing water passes through turbines.
  • The force of moving water rotates the turbines.
  • The turbines drive generators to produce electricity.
  • The controlled release of water also supports irrigation, drinking water supply, and flood management.
Applications
  • Water Supply: Reservoirs behind dams store freshwater for homes, industries, and mining sites.
  • Irrigation: Controlled water release from dams allows efficient irrigation in agriculture, boosting crop yields.
  • Hydropower: Falling water from dams is channeled to spin turbines, generating clean hydroelectric power.
  • Flood Control: Dams hold back excess water during heavy rains, preventing downstream flooding and protecting communities.
