NUCLEAR POWER PLANT (KOTA, RAJASTHAN)

(Minerals and Energy Resources)

Academic Information

Class: 10

Book Name: Contemporary India – II

Chapter: Minerals and Energy Resources

Introduction
  • The Rajasthan Atomic Power Plant (RAPP), located near Kota, Rajasthan, is a major contributor to India's nuclear energy program.
  • Nuclear power plants harness energy released from nuclear reactions to generate electricity.
  • They provide a reliable and efficient source of low-carbon energy for the nation.
Learning Objectives
  • Study the functioning of the Rajasthan Atomic Power Plant.
  • Understand its role in India's nuclear energy program and contribution to the country's energy mix.
  • Understand the safety measures and environmental considerations associated with nuclear power plants.
  • Evaluate its contribution to energy supply and regional development.
Principle
  • The Rajasthan Atomic Power Plant operates on the principle of nuclear fission.
  • During nuclear fission, the nucleus of a uranium atom splits into smaller fragments, releasing a large amount of energy.
  • The controlled chain reaction inside the reactor generates heat, which is used to produce steam.
  • The steam drives turbines connected to generators, producing electricity.
Working
  • Nuclear fuel, typically uranium, undergoes fission reactions inside the reactor core.
  • Neutrons released during fission trigger further reactions, creating a controlled chain reaction.
  • Control rods made of materials such as boron or cadmium regulate the reaction by absorbing excess neutrons.
  • The heat generated is transferred to a coolant, usually water.
  • The heated water produces steam in a steam generator.
  • The steam rotates turbines connected to generators, producing electricity.
  • The steam is then cooled and recycled for continuous operation.
Applications
  • Generation of large-scale electricity for homes, industries, and infrastructure.
  • Supports India's energy security and growing power demands.
  • Provides a reliable source of low-carbon electricity.
  • Helps reduce dependence on fossil fuels and lowers greenhouse gas emissions.
  • Contributes to sustainable energy development and economic growth.
Learning Outcome
  • Understand the working principle of a nuclear power plant.
  • Explain how nuclear fission is used to generate electricity.
  • Recognize the contribution of the Rajasthan Atomic Power Plant to India's energy sector.
  • Appreciate the importance of nuclear energy in meeting future energy requirements while maintaining environmental sustainability.
