DIGESTIVE SYSTEM

(Anatomy, Digestion & Nutrient Absorption)

Academic Information

Class: IX onwards

Topic: The Digestive System

Concepts: Digestion, Nutrient Absorption, Enzymes

About the Digestive System
  • The human digestive system is a complex network of organs and glands responsible for breaking down food, absorbing nutrients, and eliminating waste.
  • It ensures that the body receives essential nutrients while disposing of indigestible materials.
Learning Outcomes
  • Understanding Digestion: Knowledge of the digestive system aids in identifying and treating digestive disorders such as gastritis, irritable bowel syndrome (IBS), and celiac disease.
  • Educational Value: Studying the digestive system highlights the complex interactions between organs, emphasizing the importance of digestive health in overall well-being.
Structure

👄 Mouth

  • Starts digestion by chewing and mixing food with saliva.
  • Saliva contains enzymes like amylase to begin carbohydrate breakdown.

🔗 Esophagus

  • A muscular tube that moves food from the mouth to the stomach via peristalsis.

🟤 Stomach

  • Breaks down food using gastric acids and enzymes.
  • Forms a semi-liquid mixture called chyme.

🌀 Small Intestine

  • Main site for digestion and nutrient absorption.
  • Digestion is aided by enzymes from the pancreas and bile from the liver.

🟡 Pancreas

  • Secretes digestive enzymes and bicarbonate into the small intestine.
  • Also regulates blood sugar by producing insulin and glucagon.

🟫 Liver

  • Produces bile to help digest fats.
  • Processes absorbed nutrients.
  • Detoxifies harmful substances.

🟢 Gallbladder

  • Stores and releases bile into the small intestine for fat digestion.

🌀 Large Intestine

  • Absorbs water and electrolytes.
  • Forms feces for elimination.

🔘 Anus

  • Expels feces from the body.
Working
  • Food enters the mouth, where chewing and saliva begin digestion.
  • The esophagus carries food to the stomach.
  • The stomach breaks down food into a semi-liquid form called chyme.
  • The small intestine completes digestion and absorbs nutrients.
  • The large intestine absorbs water and forms waste.
  • Waste is expelled from the body through the anus.
