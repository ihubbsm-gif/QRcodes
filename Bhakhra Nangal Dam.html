BHAKHRA NANGAL DAM

(Water Resources)

Academic Information

Class: 10

Book Name: Contemporary India – II

Subject: Geography

Chapter: Water Resources

Learning Objectives
  • Understand the dam's historical significance and contribution to India's development.
  • Analyze its multipurpose benefits: irrigation, hydropower, and flood control.
  • Explore the dam's engineering marvel, design, and construction.
  • Discuss the project's social and environmental impacts.
Principle Behind Exhibit
  • The Bhakra Dam is a concrete gravity dam on the Sutlej River, using Earth's gravity to resist the water's push by the dam's mass.
Working
  • Water from the Sutlej River is stored in a large reservoir behind the Bhakra Dam.
  • The massive concrete gravity dam holds back the water using its own weight and structure.
  • The stored water is released through controlled gates and penstocks when required.
  • Flowing water rotates turbines installed in the powerhouses.
  • The turbines drive generators to produce hydroelectric power.
  • The regulated release of water supports irrigation and helps control floods in downstream areas.
  • The generated electricity is supplied to several states, supporting homes, industries, and agriculture.
Applications
  • Flood Prevention: Built to prevent floods in the Sutlej-Beas river valley.
  • Irrigation: Irrigates 10 million acres across Punjab, Himachal Pradesh, Haryana, and Rajasthan.
  • Hydropower: Two powerhouses with a combined capacity of 1,325 MW distribute electricity to multiple states.
  • Tourism: Its massive size and unique structure have made it a popular tourist attraction.
