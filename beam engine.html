BEAM ENGINE

(The Age of Industrialization)

Academic Information

Class: 8

Book Name: Our Pasts – III

Subject: History

Chapter: The Age of Industrialization

Learning Objectives
  • Understand the concept of beam engines and their historical significance during the Industrial Revolution.
  • Appreciate technological advancements by engineers like James Watt in improving beam engines.
Concept Involved
  • A beam engine is a type of steam engine where a pivoted overhead beam is used to apply force from a vertical piston to a vertical connecting rod.
  • Steam pressure drives the piston, which in turn operates a pump rod for water extraction or supply.
Working
  • Steam enters the cylinder and pushes the piston.
  • The piston moves up and down, causing the large beam to pivot about its central support.
  • The opposite end of the beam transfers this motion to a connecting rod.
  • This mechanical motion is then used to operate pumps or drive machinery.
  • The continuous movement converts the energy of steam into useful mechanical work.
Applications
  • Water Pumping: Beam engines were used to draw water out of mines, aiding in early mining operations.
  • Canal Systems: They pumped water into canals during the Industrial Revolution.
  • Cornish Engines: Specialized beam engines powered man engines, winches, and ore stamping machinery.
  • Rotative Beam Engines: Later designs directly powered mills and even steamships.
