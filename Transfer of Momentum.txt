<!-- INTRO -->

<div class="card-header">

Introduction
</div>
<div class="card-body">
<ul>
<li>Imagine playing pool. When you strike the cue ball, it transfers some of its movement (momentum) to the other balls on the table, making them roll.</li>
<li>This transfer of momentum is a fundamental principle in physics that describes how objects interact.</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>

<!-- LEARNING OUTCOMES -->
<div class="card green">
<div class="card-header">
<i class="fas fa-bullseye"></i>
Learning Outcome
</div>
<div class="card-body">
<ul>
<li>Understand the concept of momentum transfer during collisions and interactions.</li>
<li>Apply this principle to analyze and predict the outcomes of various physical scenarios.</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>

<!-- PRINCIPLE (Science Involved) -->
<div class="card purple">
<div class="card-header">
<i class="fas fa-lightbulb"></i>
Principle
</div>
<div class="card-body">
<div class="formula-box">
<p>MOMENTUM = MASS × VELOCITY</p>
</div>
<ul>
<li>Momentum is like a quantity of motion.</li>
<li>It depends on an object's mass (how much stuff it has) and its velocity (how fast and in what direction it's moving).</li>
<li>The greater the mass or velocity, the more momentum an object has.</li>
<li>The law of momentum transfer says that in a closed system (no outside forces interfere), the total momentum before an interaction is equal to the total momentum after.</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>

<!-- WORKING -->
<div class="card purple">
<div class="card-header">
<i class="fas fa-cog"></i>
Working
</div>
<div class="card-body">
<ul>
<li>When balls (1 / 2 / 3) collide, they exchange momentum according to the law of conservation of momentum.</li>
<li>Momentum transfer involves the initial and final momenta of the balls involved in the collision.</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>

<!-- APPLICATIONS -->
<div class="card pink full-width">
<div class="card-header">
<i class="fas fa-cogs"></i>
Applications
</div>
<div class="card-body">
<ul>
<li><strong>Rockets:</strong> Rockets expel gas out their back (high velocity, low mass). This transfers momentum in the opposite direction, propelling the rocket forward (low velocity, high mass).</li>
<li><strong>Airplanes:</strong> Wings push air down (high velocity, low mass) for lift, transferring momentum upwards (low velocity, high mass) to keep the plane airborne.</li>
<li><strong>Ball Games:</strong> Throwing, kicking, and hitting balls all involve transferring momentum between objects.</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>
