PINHOLE CAMERA

(Light, Optics & Image Formation)

Academic Information

Class: VI

Topic: Pinhole Camera (Camera Obscura)

Concepts: Light, Optics, Image Formation

Learning Outcome
  • Explore the basic principles of how a pinhole camera works.
  • Understand the relationship between aperture size, image formation, and exposure time.
  • Learn how light travels and forms images.
  • Appreciate the connection between science, optics, and photography.
Principle
  • The pinhole camera, also known as a Camera Obscura, is one of the simplest applications of optical principles.
  • It works on the principles of geometry and light.
  • Light travels in straight lines.
  • When light passes through a very small hole (aperture), it forms an image on the opposite surface.
  • The image formed is inverted (upside down).
  • The size of the aperture affects the clarity and brightness of the image.
Working
  • Light from an object enters through a tiny aperture.
  • The light rays pass through the small hole and project an inverted image on the opposite side of the camera.
  • Unlike modern cameras, a pinhole camera does not use lenses.
  • It relies entirely on the straight-line travel of light to form an image.
  • Smaller apertures produce sharper images but require longer exposure times.
  • Larger apertures allow more light to enter but may reduce image sharpness.
Participate & Observe
  • Observe the image formed inside the pinhole camera.
  • Notice that the image appears inverted.
  • Compare images formed using different aperture sizes.
  • Observe the effect of aperture size on image brightness and sharpness.
  • Identify how exposure time changes with different aperture sizes.
  • Relate your observations to the straight-line travel of light.
Application
  • One of the earliest image-recording devices in the history of photography.
  • Helps students understand the basic principles of light and optics.
  • Used for educational demonstrations and scientific experiments.
  • Allows photographers and enthusiasts to explore fundamental photography techniques.
  • Creates unique vintage-style images without the use of lenses.
  • Demonstrates the enduring connection between Science and Art.
  • Serves as a practical tool for learning about image formation and optical systems.
