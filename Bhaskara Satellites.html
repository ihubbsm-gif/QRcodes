BHASKARA SATELLITES

(India's First Remote Sensing Satellites)

Academic Information

Class: VIII onwards

Topic: Bhaskara Satellites

Concepts: Remote Sensing, Earth Observation, ISRO Missions

Learning Outcomes
  • Understand the objectives and significance of the Bhaskara satellite missions.
  • Learn about remote sensing and Earth observation technologies.
  • Gain knowledge of how satellite data is used for environmental monitoring and resource management.
  • Appreciate India's early achievements in space-based Earth observation.
Science Explained

🛰️ Bhaskara Satellites

  • Bhaskara-I was launched on 7 June 1979 and Bhaskara-II on 20 November 1981 by ISRO.
  • They were India's first experimental remote sensing satellites.
  • Named after the renowned Indian mathematicians Bhāskara I and Bhāskara II.

🎯 Mission Objectives

  • Conduct Earth observation experiments related to meteorology, hydrology, forestry, and geology.
  • Study ocean conditions and atmospheric water content.

🔬 Scientific Instruments

  • Television Cameras operating in visible band (600 nm) and near-infrared band (800 nm).
  • Satellite Microwave Radiometer (SAMIR) operating at 19 GHz and 22 GHz for studying ocean surface conditions, water vapour, and liquid water content in the atmosphere.
Working and Observation

⚙️ Working

  • The satellites were spin-stabilized and placed in Low Earth Orbit (LEO).
  • Television cameras captured images of Earth's land and water surfaces.
  • The SAMIR instrument measured atmospheric and oceanic parameters using microwave radiation.

🔭 Observation

  • Bhaskara-I and Bhaskara-II collected more than 2,000 images of Earth's surface and atmosphere.
  • The data helped scientists study land resources, forest cover, water bodies, ocean conditions, and atmospheric moisture.
Applications
  • Agriculture: Monitoring crop conditions and land use.
  • Forestry: Assessing forest resources and environmental changes.
  • Hydrology: Studying rivers, reservoirs, and water management.
  • Geology: Mapping geological features and natural resources.
  • Environmental Monitoring: Tracking changes in land, water, and climate conditions.
  • Remote Sensing Development: Laid the foundation for India's future Earth observation and remote sensing satellite programmes.
Interesting Fact
  • The Bhaskara satellites were India's first major step into remote sensing technology, paving the way for advanced satellite missions such as IRS, Cartosat, Oceansat, and Resourcesat. 🇮🇳🛰️
