SOURCES OF POWER

(Minerals and Energy Resources)

Academic Information

Class: 10

Book Name: Contemporary India – II

Chapter: Minerals and Energy Resources

Learning Objective
  • Understand the various sources of power used for generating electricity, including renewable and non-renewable energy sources.
  • Explore the principles behind each type of power generation.
  • Understand their applications and environmental and economic impacts.
Principle Behind Exhibit
  • The exhibit demonstrates the various sources from which we derive power to run various applications.
  • It emphasizes the differences in technology, efficiency, and sustainability among different energy sources.
☀️ SOLAR POWER
Definition
  • Solar power is the conversion of sunlight into electricity using two main technologies:
  • Photovoltaics (PV)
  • Concentrated Solar Power (CSP)
Learning Objectives
  • Understand the basic principles of PV and CSP technologies.
  • Learn about the photoelectric effect and how it facilitates solar energy conversion.
Applications
  • Rooftop solar panels for homes and businesses to generate clean electricity.
  • Solar farms for large-scale electricity production.
  • Portable solar chargers for off-grid applications, providing energy in remote areas.
Working
  • Solar panels absorb sunlight and convert it into electrical energy through the photovoltaic effect.
  • The generated electricity can be used directly or stored in batteries.
Participate & Observe
  • Observe the solar panel model.
  • Identify how sunlight is converted into electricity.
  • Discuss the advantages of solar energy as a renewable source.
🌬️ WIND POWER
Definition
  • Wind power is the conversion of wind energy into electricity using wind turbines or into mechanical power using windmills or wind pumps.
Learning Objectives
  • Study the technology and mechanics behind modern wind turbines.
  • Explore historical uses and advancements of windmills and wind pumps.
Applications
  • Wind farms for renewable electricity generation.
  • Historical windmills for mechanical power in agricultural applications.
  • Wind pumps for water pumping in rural areas, contributing to sustainable farming practices.
Working
  • Wind causes the turbine blades to rotate.
  • The rotating blades drive a generator that produces electricity.
Participate & Observe
  • Observe the movement of the turbine blades.
  • Identify how wind energy is converted into electrical energy.
  • Compare wind energy with other renewable energy sources.
