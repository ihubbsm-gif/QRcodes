Learning Objectives
  • Understand the operational principles of Pelton wheel turbines.
  • Learn the concept of impulse force and energy conversion.
  • Explore the design and components of Pelton turbines.
  • Study bucket geometry and nozzle configurations.
  • Analyze factors affecting turbine efficiency and performance.
  • Understand the role of jet velocity and water flow dynamics.
  • Learn about the applications of Pelton turbines in hydropower generation.
  • Explore their use in micro-hydro systems and educational demonstrations.
  • Recognize adaptations for different environmental and operational conditions.
Principle of Pelton Wheel Turbine
  • Operates on the principle of impulse.
  • High-velocity water jets strike the turbine buckets.
  • The water's kinetic energy is converted into mechanical energy.
  • The buckets deflect the water, causing the wheel to rotate.
  • The rotational energy is transferred through a shaft.
  • The shaft drives machinery or electrical generators.
Working
  • Water is directed through a nozzle at high speed.
  • The water jet strikes the turbine buckets.
  • The force of the water rotates the wheel.
  • The rotating shaft transfers mechanical energy.
  • The connected generator produces electricity.
  • The used water flows away after striking the buckets.
Applications
  • Used in high-head hydropower plants.
  • Generates electricity from high-velocity water sources.
  • Suitable for mountain streams and dams.
  • Used in micro-hydro power systems.
  • Provides sustainable energy in remote areas.
  • Used for educational demonstrations.
  • Helps explain impulse turbine principles in hydropower engineering.
