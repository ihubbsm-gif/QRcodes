PROPERTIES OF LIGHT

(Reflection, Refraction & Diffraction)

Academic Information

Class: VII onwards

Topic: Properties of Light

Concepts: Reflection, Refraction, Diffraction, Optical Elements

Learning Outcome
  • Investigate the fundamental properties of light propagation.
  • Understand the concepts of reflection, refraction, and diffraction.
  • Analyze how these properties affect the behavior of light in different media.
  • Observe the working of various optical elements and their applications.
Principle
  • Reflection: Bouncing back of light after striking a surface.
  • Refraction: Bending of light when it passes from one medium to another.
  • Diffraction: Bending or spreading of light around obstacles or through small openings.
  • Different optical elements interact with light in different ways, producing various optical effects.
Working

🔲 1. Plane Glass

  • A beam of light passes through plane glass with little or no deviation.
  • Demonstrates the transmission of light through a transparent medium.

🪞 2. Plane Mirror

  • Light reflects from the mirror surface.
  • The angle of incidence is equal to the angle of reflection.

🔍 3. Concave Mirror

  • Light rays parallel to the principal axis converge at a focal point after reflection.
  • Produces magnified or inverted images depending on object position.

🔍 4. Convex Mirror

  • Light rays diverge after reflection.
  • Provides a wider field of view.

🔎 5. Concave Lens

  • Light rays diverge after passing through the lens.
  • Forms virtual, erect, and diminished images.

🔎 6. Convex Lens

  • Light rays converge after passing through the lens.
  • Can produce magnified or real images.

🌈 7. Prism

  • Refracts light and separates white light into its constituent colours.
  • Demonstrates dispersion of light.
Applications
  • Mirrors are used in vehicles, telescopes, and daily-life applications.
  • Lenses are used in spectacles, cameras, microscopes, and telescopes.
  • Prisms are used in optical instruments and scientific experiments.
  • Understanding light helps in designing optical and imaging systems.
  • These principles form the foundation of photography, communication, medicine, and modern optics.
