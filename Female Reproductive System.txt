<!-- STRUCTURE -->
<div class="card purple full-width">
<div class="card-header">
<i class="fas fa-shapes"></i>
Structure
</div>
<div class="card-body">

<div class="part-box">
<h4>🥚 Ovaries</h4>
<ul>
<li>Two almond-shaped organs located in the pelvis.</li>
<li>Produce eggs (ova).</li>
<li>Secrete hormones including estrogen and progesterone.</li>
<li>These hormones regulate the menstrual cycle and support pregnancy.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="part-box">
<h4>➡️ Fallopian Tubes (Oviducts)</h4>
<ul>
<li>Tubes extending from the ovaries to the uterus.</li>
<li>Capture and transport the egg from the ovary to the uterus.</li>
<li>Site where fertilization typically occurs.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="part-box">
<h4>🏠 Uterus (Womb)</h4>
<ul>
<li>A hollow, muscular organ where a fertilized egg implants and develops into a fetus.</li>
<li>Has three layers: Endometrium (inner lining), Myometrium (muscular middle layer), and Perimetrium (outer layer).</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="part-box">
<h4>🔘 Cervix</h4>
<ul>
<li>The lower, narrow part of the uterus that connects to the vagina.</li>
<li>Acts as a barrier between the vagina and uterus.</li>
<li>Produces mucus that changes in consistency throughout the menstrual cycle.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="part-box">
<h4>🔗 Vagina</h4>
<ul>
<li>A muscular, elastic tube that connects the external genitalia to the uterus.</li>
<li>Serves as the passage for menstrual flow, sexual intercourse, and childbirth.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="part-box">
<h4>📍 External Genitalia</h4>
<ul>
<li>Includes Labia Majora, Labia Minora, Clitoris, and Vaginal Opening.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="part-box">
<h4>🩷 Labia Majora and Labia Minora</h4>
<ul>
<li>Folds of skin surrounding the vaginal opening.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="part-box">
<h4>🔆 Clitoris</h4>
<ul>
<li>A sensitive organ involved in sexual arousal.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="part-box">
<h4>🔘 Vaginal Opening</h4>
<ul>
<li>Opening that connects the vagina to the external environment.</li>
</ul>
</div>
