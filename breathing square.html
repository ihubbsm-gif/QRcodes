BREATHING SQUARE

(Optical Illusion & Visual Perception)

Academic Information

Class: VII onwards

Topic: Optical Illusions & Visual Perception

Concepts: Geometry, Symmetry, Spatial Relationships

Learning Outcomes
  • Explain how the Breathing Square demonstrates optical illusions.
  • Understand the principles of visual perception.
  • Apply the concepts of optical illusions to practical situations.
  • Analyze and evaluate visual effects in different fields.
  • Develop an understanding of perception and spatial relationships.
Science Involved
  • The visual effect of expansion and contraction is an optical illusion.
  • The illusion is created by the arrangement and movement of geometric shapes.
  • The brain interprets the changing visual patterns as expansion or contraction.
  • The effect demonstrates how human visual perception can be influenced.
Working and Observation
  • The setup consists of four equal-sized square pieces separated by narrow gaps.
  • A red square is placed beneath the top layer.
  • The centres of the four square quadrants align with the centre of the red square.
  • When the red square rotates by 90°, it creates the illusion of expansion.
  • When it rotates by 180°, it creates the illusion of contraction.
  • The actual size of the squares remains unchanged throughout the motion.
Applications
  • Art Installations: Creates visually engaging and dynamic experiences.
  • Virtual Reality (VR): Enhances immersion and visual effects.
  • Psychological Research: Helps study visual perception and spatial relationships.
  • Product Design: Creates eye-catching packaging, logos, and branding materials.
  • Educational Tools: Demonstrates concepts of geometry, symmetry, and visual perception.
  • Marketing and Advertising: Captures attention and creates memorable visual experiences.
