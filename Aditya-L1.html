ADITYA-L1

(India's First Dedicated Solar Mission)

Academic Information

Class: IV onwards

Topic: Aditya-L1 Mission

Concepts: Heliophysics, Lagrange Point, Space Weather

Learning Outcomes
  • Understand the objectives and significance of the Aditya-L1 mission.
  • Learn about the Sun's structure, including the corona, photosphere, and solar wind.
  • Gain knowledge about space weather and its effects on Earth.
  • Appreciate India's achievements in solar research and space exploration.
Science Explained: Aditya-L1 Mission
  • Aditya-L1 is India's first dedicated solar mission launched by ISRO.
  • The mission studies the Sun, particularly the Corona (outermost layer of the Sun), Photosphere (visible surface of the Sun), and Solar Wind (stream of charged particles emitted by the Sun).
  • It aims to understand coronal heating mechanisms, solar wind acceleration, the origin of solar flares and Coronal Mass Ejections (CMEs), and the Sun's influence on space weather.
Working and Observation

⚙️ Working

  • Aditya-L1 is positioned at Lagrange Point 1 (L1), about 1.5 million km from Earth.
  • This location allows continuous observation of the Sun without interruptions caused by eclipses.
  • The spacecraft carries seven scientific payloads, including the Visible Emission Line Coronagraph (VELC), Solar Ultraviolet Imaging Telescope (SUIT), and spectrometers and particle analyzers for studying solar radiation and particles.

🔬 Observation

  • Provides valuable data about the Sun's corona and photosphere, solar wind behaviour, Coronal Mass Ejections (CMEs), and solar flares and plasma activity.
  • Helps scientists understand why the corona is much hotter than the Sun's surface.
  • Contributes to accurate monitoring and prediction of space weather events.
Applications
  • Enhances scientific understanding of the Sun and the field of Heliophysics.
  • Helps predict solar storms and space weather that can affect satellites, communication systems, GPS navigation, and power grids on Earth.
  • Supports the protection of space-based technologies and future space missions.
  • Demonstrates India's growing capabilities in advanced space research and solar observation.
Interesting Fact
  • Aditya-L1 is the first Indian mission dedicated exclusively to studying the Sun and continuously observes it from the strategically located L1 point, where the gravitational forces of the Earth and Sun are balanced. ☀️🇮🇳
