<!-- SCIENCE INVOLVED -->
<div class="card blue full-width">
<div class="card-header">
<i class="fas fa-lightbulb"></i>
Science Involved
</div>
<div class="card-body">
<ul>
<li>Sound is produced by vibrations.</li>
<li>Vibrations create sound waves that travel through a material medium.</li>
<li>When guitar or violin strings are plucked, or a drum is struck, vibrations produce sound.</li>
<li>Sound waves require a material medium for propagation.</li>
</ul>
<div class="medium-box">
<div class="medium-item">
<h4>🪨 Solids</h4>
<p>Sound travels fastest because molecules are closely packed.</p>
</div>
<div class="medium-item">
<h4>💧 Liquids</h4>
<p>Sound travels slower than in solids.</p>
</div>
<div class="medium-item">
<h4>💨 Gases</h4>
<p>Sound travels slowest due to greater molecular spacing.</p>
</div>
</div>
</div>
</div>

<!-- WORKING & OBSERVATION -->
<div class="card orange">
<div class="card-header">
<i class="fas fa-eye"></i>
Working and Observation
</div>
<div class="card-body">
<ul>
<li>Observe the vibration of the string in the model.</li>
<li>Notice how vibrations travel through the string.</li>
<li>Compare how sound propagates through different materials.</li>
<li>Understand that sound cannot travel without a material medium.</li>
<li>Relate the observed vibrations to the sounds produced by musical instruments.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<!-- APPLICATIONS -->
<div class="card pink full-width">
<div class="card-header">
<i class="fas fa-cogs"></i>
Applications
</div>
<div class="card-body">

<div class="app-box">
<h4>🎵 Musical Instruments</h4>
<ul>
<li>Vibrations produce sound in guitars, pianos, flutes, violins, and drums.</li>
<li>Sound waves are used in recording and reproducing music and voice.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="app-box">
<h4>📻 Radio & Telecommunication</h4>
<ul>
<li>Sound waves are converted into electrical signals for telephone communication.</li>
<li>Used in radio and television broadcasting.</li>
<li>Mobile phones transmit voice and data using sound-related communication technologies.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="app-box">
<h4>🏥 Medical Applications</h4>
<ul>
<li><strong>Ultrasound:</strong> Produces images of internal organs, tissues, and blood flow.</li>
<li><strong>Sonography:</strong> Visualizes developing fetuses during pregnancy.</li>
<li><strong>Echocardiography:</strong> Examines the structure and function of the heart.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="app-box">
<h4>🔍 Non-Destructive Testing (NDT)</h4>
<ul>
<li>Inspects materials without causing damage.</li>
<li>Detects defects in manufactured products.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="app-box">
<h4>🌊 Underwater Sonar</h4>
<ul>
<li>Maps the ocean floor.</li>
<li>Detects and locates underwater objects.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="app-box">
<h4>🌍 Other Applications</h4>
<ul>
<li>Seismology: Uses sound waves generated by earthquakes to study Earth's interior.</li>
<li>Noise Pollution Measurement: Measures noise levels in urban and industrial environments.</li>
<li>Sonar and Radar, Smoke Detectors, Intrusion Alarms.</li>
</ul>
</div>
