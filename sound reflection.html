SOUND REFLECTORS

(Sound)

Academic Information

Class: 10

Book Name: Science

Chapter No: 11

Chapter: Sound

Learning Objectives
  • Demonstrate how parabolic surfaces focus sound waves, linking geometric principles from optics to acoustics.
  • Allow hands-on exploration of wave behavior and real-world applications like microphones.
Science Involved
  • Two large metallic parabolic reflectors are placed at each end of the gallery, with metallic rings indicating their foci.
  • When you speak into the ring, your sound waves are reflected back parallel and converge at the focus of the opposite reflector.
  • This process makes your voice clearly heard at the center of the ring.
Participate & Observe
  • Speak gently near the metallic ring of one reflector and let your friend listen to your voice at the ring of the other reflector at the end of the gallery.
  • Your friend will hear your voice so far and so clearly without any electrical or mechanical connection between the two reflectors.
Applications
  • Microphones: Parabolic sound reflectors focus sound onto the diaphragm, improving sensitivity.
  • Hearing Aids: They amplify sound to the ear canal, enhancing hearing.
  • Loudspeakers: Reflectors project sound over wide areas for better distribution.
  • Radar Antennas: Similar principles are used to focus radio waves.
  • Whispering Galleries: Architectural designs allow whispers to be transmitted across large rooms.
  • Energy Harvesting: Reflectors can collect ambient noise vibrations for energy production.
