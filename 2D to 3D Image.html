2D TO 3D IMAGE

(Visual Perception & Persistence of Vision)

Academic Information

Class: VI, VII & VIII

Subject: Science

Concepts: Motion Perception, Depth Perception, Persistence of Vision

Learning Outcomes
  • Understand how visual illusions create a perception of depth in a two-dimensional image.
  • Analyze the mechanisms that trick the brain into seeing three-dimensional objects.
  • Explore the concepts of motion perception, depth perception, and persistence of vision.
  • Relate these concepts to real-world applications in science and technology.
Science Explained
  • The illusion of a fast-rotating 2D circle or triangle appearing as a 3D sphere or pyramid is based on our perception of motion and shape.
  • The brain uses motion and visual cues to create a sense of depth.
  • This demonstrates how the human visual system constructs three-dimensional images from limited sensory information.
Working
  • A two-dimensional object such as a circle or triangle is rotated rapidly.
  • The eyes receive a series of continuously changing images.
  • Due to persistence of vision, these images blend together and appear continuous.
  • The brain combines the motion with its understanding of depth and form.
  • The flat object appears to gain depth and volume, resembling a three-dimensional shape such as a sphere or pyramid.
  • This illusion shows how the brain interprets visual information to construct reality.
Participate & Observe
  • Observe the appearance of the object before and during rotation.
  • Notice how a flat 2D shape appears to transform into a 3D object.
  • Compare the visual effect at different rotation speeds.
  • Identify the motion cues that create the illusion of depth.
  • Discuss why the brain perceives the rotating object as three-dimensional.
  • Record and compare observations with classmates.
Applications
  • Used by scientists to create three-dimensional models from two-dimensional data.
  • Helps in scientific research, analysis, and visualization.
  • Used in computer graphics, animation, and visual effects.
  • Enhances the immersive experience in movies and video games.
  • Applied in virtual reality, simulations, and educational models.
  • Helps researchers study human visual perception and brain processing.
