<!-- SCIENCE EXPLAINED -->
<div class="card blue">
<div class="card-header">
<i class="fas fa-lightbulb"></i>
Science Explained
</div>
<div class="card-body">
<ul>
<li>The iris controls the size of the pupil in response to light.</li>
<li>It regulates the amount of light entering the eye.</li>
<li>In bright light, the pupil becomes smaller to reduce light intake.</li>
<li>In dim light, the pupil becomes larger to allow more light to enter.</li>
<li>This automatic adjustment helps the eye adapt to different lighting conditions.</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>

<!-- WORKING & OBSERVATION -->
<div class="card orange full-width">
<div class="card-header">
<i class="fas fa-eye"></i>
Working and Observation
</div>
<div class="card-body">
<p>Observe how the iris changes the pupil's diameter based on surrounding light conditions.</p>
<div class="light-grid">

<div class="light-box dim">
<h4>🌙 Low-Light Conditions</h4>
<ul>
<li>The iris muscles relax.</li>
<li>The pupil dilates (enlarges).</li>
<li>More light enters the eye.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="light-box bright">
<h4>☀️ Bright-Light Conditions</h4>
<ul>
<li>The iris muscles contract.</li>
<li>The pupil constricts (becomes smaller).</li>
<li>Less light enters the eye.</li>
</ul>
</div>

</div>
<p style="margin-top:15px;">The model demonstrates pupil contraction and dilation under different light intensities. Students can observe this effect in real time by looking at their own pupils in a mirror under different lighting conditions.</p>
